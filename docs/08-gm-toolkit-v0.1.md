# GM Toolkit v0.1

This section gives the GM practical tools for running **Dark Heresy Spire** at the table.

The core game already defines acolytes, rolls, fallout, Patron pressure, Heat, and Mission / Operation / Situation structure. This toolkit turns those pieces into procedures for prep, consequences, threats, factions, clues, clocks, and table-facing decisions.

The GM is not here to defeat the acolytes.

The GM is here to make the Imperium, the enemy, the Patron, and the consequences of truth push back.

---

## 1. GM Agenda

When running the game, aim to:

1. **Make the acolytes competent.**
2. **Make the truth dangerous.**
3. **Make authority useful and costly.**
4. **Make every success change the situation.**
5. **Make fallout create story, not dead ends.**
6. **Make the Patron necessary, frightening, and compromised.**
7. **Make the Imperium feel too large to fully control.**
8. **Make combat costly for the unprepared.** Acolytes who fight without stacking advantages first should pay in stress, Fallot, and clocks. Acolytes who prepare properly should feel the payoff in every roll.

Acolytes should not feel like ordinary investigators.

They should feel like deniable agents of a terrifying power who can break doors, ruin lives, command officials, burn cults, and still be swallowed by the machinery they serve.

---

## 2. GM Principles

### Ask What It Costs

When an acolyte acts, ask:

- What does this reveal?
- Who notices?
- What evidence remains?
- What institution gets involved?
- What does the enemy learn?
- What does the Patron think this means?
- What Resistance is being risked?

The answer is usually more important than whether the action “works.”

### Do Not Hide the Core Clue

If a clue is necessary for the Mission to continue, the acolytes should get it.

The roll decides:

- How late they find it.
- Who sees them find it.
- What they misunderstand.
- What stress they take.
- What evidence they leave.
- What enemy action it triggers.

Failure should complicate the investigation, not end it.

### Let Authority Work

When the cell invokes power, let it work.

Then ask what that power costs.

A Rosette, writ, seal, Inquisitorial cipher, Patron phrase, or official threat should open doors. It should also create witnesses, resentment, reports, factional complaint, Patron Notice, Heat, or a future rival.

### Make the Enemy Specific

Do not run “a cult” as a vague evil cloud.

Give the opposition:

- A leader.
- A need.
- A method.
- A vulnerability.
- A lie.
- A reason someone protects them.
- A response when the cell interferes.

### Push with Consequences, Not Turns

Enemies do not need traditional turns.

They act when:

- A player rolls 7 or lower.
- Heat reaches a threshold.
- A clock fills.
- The cell ignores them.
- Fallout gives them an opening.
- The fiction demands immediate response.

## 3. When to Call for a Roll

Call for a roll only when all three are true:

1. The action is dangerous, uncertain, opposed, exposed, or morally costly.
2. Failure or partial success would change the situation.
3. Stress, fallout, Heat, Awareness, or a clock could result.

Do not roll for ordinary competence.

Acolytes are trained, dangerous, and chosen by the Inquisition.

Roll when their competence risks something.

### Good Reasons to Roll

| Situation | Possible Roll |
|---|---|
| Entering a guarded shrine under false papers | Deceive + Ecclesiarchy |
| Reading a heretical pattern in tithe records | Investigate + Heresy |
| Commanding local enforcers without revealing the Inquisition | Command + Imperium |
| Sneaking through a manufactorum lockdown | Skulk + Mechanicus |
| Resisting a daemon’s whispered bargain | Resist + Warp |
| Requisitioning restricted ammunition under cover | Procure + Militarum |
| Escaping a collapsing hab-block | Move + Frontier |
| Interrogating a cult courier without killing them | Command or Investigate + Heresy |

### Poor Reasons to Roll

Do not roll just to see if an acolyte:

- Knows basic Imperial law if they have Imperium.
- Can fire a weapon in ordinary conditions.
- Can intimidate a helpless civilian.
- Can find an obvious document after securing the room.
- Can enter a location where their Cover obviously applies.
- Can perform routine duties from their former Allegiance.

Instead, say what happens and move forward.

## 4. Setting Difficulty

Difficulty reflects how hostile, exposed, unstable, or overwhelming the situation is.

| Difficulty | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Safe** | The action is risky only because something is at stake. The acolyte has position, leverage, or plausible cover. |
| **Risky** | The situation is watched, unstable, contested, time-sensitive, or socially dangerous. |
| **Dangerous** | The situation is actively hostile, exposed, supernatural, heavily defended, or likely to change the Operation. |
| **Impossible** | The action should not work cleanly. Even success brings stress, exposure, fallout, sacrifice, or escalation. |

### Difficulty Probabilities and Calibration

Difficulty is the strongest lever in the dice system because it changes which result bands are possible.

Use Difficulty to tune the **chance of each result band**. Use stress dice to tune the **severity of cost**. Use Heat or Enemy Awareness to decide **who notices**. Use clocks for **delayed consequences**.

Do not make every dangerous moment Dangerous difficulty, D10 stress, Heat +2, Awareness +1, and a clock tick. Pick the pressure that best expresses the fiction.

| Pool | Difficulty | Critical Failure 1 | Failure 2-5 | Success at Cost 6-7 | Clean Success 8-9 | Critical Success 10 |
|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2D10 | Safe | 1.0% | 24.0% | 24.0% | 32.0% | 19.0% |
| 2D10 | Risky | 19.0% | 56.0% | 16.0% | 8.0% | 1.0% |
| 2D10 | Dangerous | 100.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 0.0% |
| 3D10 | Safe | 0.1% | 12.4% | 21.8% | 38.6% | 27.1% |
| 3D10 | Risky | 2.8% | 47.2% | 28.4% | 18.8% | 2.8% |
| 3D10 | Dangerous | 27.1% | 60.4% | 9.8% | 2.6% | 0.1% |
| 4D10 | Safe | 0.0% | 6.2% | 17.8% | 41.6% | 34.4% |
| 4D10 | Risky | 0.4% | 30.9% | 33.9% | 29.6% | 5.2% |
| 4D10 | Dangerous | 5.2% | 63.5% | 22.9% | 8.0% | 0.4% |
| 5D10 | Safe | 0.0% | 3.1% | 13.7% | 42.2% | 41.0% |
| 5D10 | Risky | 0.0% | 18.7% | 34.1% | 39.0% | 8.1% |
| 5D10 | Dangerous | 0.9% | 49.1% | 33.7% | 15.5% | 0.9% |
| 6D10 | Safe | 0.0% | 1.6% | 10.2% | 41.4% | 46.9% |
| 6D10 | Risky | 0.0% | 10.9% | 31.1% | 46.6% | 11.4% |
| 6D10 | Dangerous | 0.1% | 34.2% | 40.1% | 24.0% | 1.6% |

Read the table this way:

- **Safe** tests competence under stakes. Skilled acolytes usually succeed, and the main question is whether they pay a cost.
- **Risky** tests preparation, cover, leverage, or pressure. A 3D10 Risky roll is a coin flip between failure and success.
- **Dangerous** tests whether the cell built enough advantage to survive the approach. A 5D10 Dangerous roll is a coin flip.
- **Impossible** is not a probability category. It means the action cannot be clean: even success brings stress, exposure, fallout, sacrifice, or escalation.

Low pools need special care:

- A **1D10 Risky** or **1D10 Dangerous** roll becomes an automatic critical failure because all dice are removed.
- A **2D10 Dangerous** roll also becomes an automatic critical failure.
- If an acolyte only has **1D10**, invite them to get help, use gear, change approach, find leverage, or accept automatic success with a clear cost instead of forcing a dead-end roll.

### Default Difficulty by Approach

| Approach | Default Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Strong cover, clear leverage, prepared position, or ordinary access | **Safe** |
| Scrutiny, time pressure, weak cover, contested access, or an unstable scene | **Risky** |
| Alert opposition, exposed methods, hostile ground, supernatural danger, forced to rely on a negative equipment aspect, or active pursuit | **Dangerous** |
| Success cannot be clean without sacrifice, exposure, fallout, or escalation | **Impossible** |

### When Negative Equipment Aspects Apply

A negative aspect increases Difficulty by 1 only when the fiction makes the item’s weakness unavoidable. This is not a routine penalty.

Invoke a negative aspect when:

- The acolyte is using the item outside its designed role (a Concealable pistol at extreme range, a Holy weapon to deceive).
- The environment turns a tag against the user (a Loud weapon during infiltration, a Traceable tool under Mechanicus audit).
- The opposition has specifically prepared against the item’s strength, forcing reliance on its weakness.
- The acolyte has no better option and must lean on the one thing the item does badly.

Do not invoke a negative aspect when the acolyte is using the item within its intended purpose. A boltgun’s Loud tag does not increase Difficulty during an open firefight — it increases Difficulty when the acolyte needs the firefight to stay quiet.

### Calibrated Examples

| Situation | Likely Pool and Difficulty | Why |
|---|---|---|
| A Legate uses **Command + Inquisition** to imply limited Patron authority before a nervous local official. | 3D10 Safe or Risky | Safe if the official wants direction; Risky if the office is already auditing the cell. |
| A Penumbra uses **Skulk + Underworld + Cover Papers** to enter a gang counting-house during normal business. | 4D10 Safe | The cover and local domain fit, so the roll tests whether anything is left behind. |
| A Secutor uses **Fight + Militarum + Mastery** to ambush alerted cult guards during a Hunted Operation. | 4D10 Risky or Dangerous | Risky if the ambush is solid; Dangerous if the guards are already watching for the cell's methods. |
| An Explicator uses **Investigate + Heresy** on a contaminated confession while the enemy is moving evidence. | 3D10 Risky | The clue should appear, but the likely costs are time, stress, or false context. |
| A Sanctioned Psyker uses **Investigate + Warp** to read a fresh possession trace in a warded shrine. | 3D10 Dangerous | The power can work, but the place, trace, and Warp risk make clean success unlikely. |
| The cell tries quiet extraction at Heat 9 while local law, rivals, and the enemy all watch the exits. | Usually Dangerous or Impossible | Quiet success may require changing the situation, burning Cover, or accepting a major cost. |

### Difficulty Questions

Ask:

1. Is the opposition already alert?
2. Is the location monitored or sacred?
3. Does the cell’s Cover actually explain this?
4. Would failure expose the Inquisition?
5. Is the action being done under time pressure?
6. Is the action physically, spiritually, or politically dangerous?
7. Is forbidden knowledge, psychic power, or xenos influence involved?
8. Has Heat made this approach harder?

### Heat and Difficulty

Heat does not make every roll harder.

Heat makes exposed rolls harder.

| Heat State | Difficulty Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Ghosts** | Covert actions are usually Safe if the Cover fits. Loud action may still be Risky. |
| **Rumors** | Cover, access, social, and stealth rolls may become Risky when scrutiny matters. |
| **Hunted** | Covert action against alerted targets is often Risky or Dangerous. |
| **Burned** | Quiet approaches are usually Dangerous or impossible until the cell changes the situation. |

A firefight during a Burned Operation might be Safe if the Secutor has surprise and position.

Sneaking out afterward is probably Dangerous.

## 5. Assigning Stress

When a roll causes stress, choose the Resistance that best represents what is being risked.

| Resistance | Use When the Cost Is… |
|---|---|
| **Body** | Injury, fatigue, poison, radiation, disease, deprivation, pain |
| **Mind** | Fear, guilt, memory damage, obsession, trauma, doubt |
| **Shadow** | Exposure, cover damage, witnesses, evidence, surveillance, aliases |
| **Authority** | Faction standing, Patron trust, official pressure, chain of command |
| **Corruption** | Warp-taint, forbidden knowledge, xenos contamination, mutation, possession |

### Stress Die Guidelines

| Stress Die | Use For |
|---|---|
| **D3** | Minor pressure, brief risk, contained exposure, soft harm |
| **D6** | Serious trouble, armed threat, meaningful scrutiny, hard choices |
| **D8** | Major danger, restricted enemies, psychic danger, public scandal |
| **D10** | Daemonhosts, lethal ambushes, sector politics, extreme corruption, open Inquisitorial disaster |

### Mixed Stress

Some consequences threaten more than one Resistance.

Choose one primary Resistance, then optionally add a smaller secondary stress.

Examples:

| Situation | Stress |
|---|---|
| Public firefight in a noble district | D6 Body and D3 Shadow |
| Reading a daemonological confession | D6 Mind or D6 Corruption |
| Flashing the Rosette before a hostile governor | D6 Authority or D6 Shadow |
| Torturing a useful but innocent witness | D6 Mind or D6 Authority |
| Using xenotech to bypass a sealed vault | D6 Corruption and Heat +1 |

Do not overuse mixed stress. Save it for moments where two kinds of cost are clearly present.

## 6. GM Moves

Use GM Moves when the players roll 7 or lower, when Heat reaches a threshold, when Fallout triggers, when a clock fills, or when the fiction demands pressure.

### Soft Moves

Soft Moves warn, reveal, complicate, or offer a choice.

| Move | Example |
|---|---|
| Show evidence of danger | “The cherubim stops singing and begins recording.” |
| Reveal a cost | “The clerk can get you access, but only if she logs your seal.” |
| Put someone in a spot | “Your Contact is at the checkpoint with a gun to his head.” |
| Offer a worse bargain | “You can get the prisoner out now, but not quietly.” |
| Show institutional pressure | “The Arbites captain asks whose authority supersedes hers.” |
| Threaten Cover | “The real tithe auditor arrives tomorrow.” |
| Tick a clock | “The cult moves the shipment one step closer to the ritual site.” |
| Separate the cell | “The elevator accepts only one credential at a time.” |
| Reveal a false lead | “The ledger points to a scapegoat who is too obvious.” |
| Ask who suffers | “Someone saw you. Which Contact is now at risk?” |

### Hard Moves

Hard Moves land consequences.

| Move | Example |
|---|---|
| Inflict stress | “Take D8 Body from the heavy stubber fire.” |
| Trigger Fallout | “Your Cover does not just fail. It becomes evidence.” |
| Raise Heat | “The witnesses scatter. Heat +1.” |
| Raise Enemy Awareness | “The courier escapes with your faces. Awareness +1.” |
| Burn an asset | “The safehouse is no longer safe.” |
| Damage Cover | “Your Administratum identity is now Flagged.” |
| Force a choice | “Save the witness or secure the artifact.” |
| Escalate the enemy | “The cult accelerates the rite.” |
| Involve a faction | “The Mechanicus claims jurisdiction over the corpse.” |
| Mark Patron Notice | “Your Patron hears about the firefight before your report.” |
| Fill a clock | “The Arbites cordon locks down the district.” |
| Change the objective | “The quiet extraction is no longer possible.” |

## 7. Threat Design

Threats in this game are not stat blocks first.

They are pressure engines.

A threat should answer:

1. What do they want?
2. What do they do if ignored?
3. What protects them?
4. What reveals them?
5. What do they know?
6. What do they fear?
7. What can the cell gain from them?
8. What happens when they are exposed?

### Threat Template

```text
Name:
Type:
Objective:
Instinct:
Tell:
Difficulty:
Resistance:
Stress Die:
Protection:
Tags:
Clue / Resource:
What They Know:
What They Fear:
What Protects Them:
What They Do If Ignored:
What They Do At Heat 3:
What They Do At Heat 6:
What They Do At Heat 9:
```

### Threat Traits

| Trait | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | Safe, Risky, Dangerous, or Impossible to act against. |
| **Resistance** | Stress needed to defeat, expose, capture, silence, or banish. |
| **Stress Die** | Stress inflicted when the threat pushes back. |
| **Protection** | Stress ignored, absorbed, or redirected. |
| **Instinct** | What the threat naturally does when not stopped. |
| **Tell** | What reveals its nature. |
| **Clue / Resource** | What the cell can gain from it. |

### Inflicting Stress on Threats and Obstacles

When an acolyte inflicts stress to overcome a threat or obstacle, the stress die is determined by the weapon or equipment used (often matching its Resource die). Without a relevant piece of equipment, the stress die is **D3**, assuming the action is possible at all.

### Threat Scale

| Scale | Resistance | Stress Die | Use For |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| **Minor** | 3–5 | D3 / D6 | Informant, clerk, ganger, cult courier |
| **Standard** | 6–10 | D6 | Cult enforcer, corrupt official, trained guard |
| **Major** | 10–15 | D8 | Magos, noble conspirator, witch, daemonhost vessel |
| **Severe** | 15–20+ | D8 / D10 | Greater daemon sign, xenos patriarch, sector conspiracy node |

Resistance does not always mean physical durability.

It may represent secrecy, loyalty, legal insulation, ritual protection, public reputation, bodyguards, wards, or institutional inertia.

## 8. Opposition Types

### Cult Cell

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Growth, secrecy, sacrifice, revenge, revelation, liberation, power |
| **Protects Itself With** | Compartmentalization, coded rites, blackmail, false charities |
| **Tells** | Repeated symbols, missing people, odd tithes, shared dreams |
| **Pushback** | Ambushes, false confessions, ritual acceleration, martyrdom |

Example moves:

- Move the ritual site.
- Sacrifice a disposable member.
- Frame a loyal Imperial servant.
- Turn a witness into bait.
- Reveal that the cult solved a real local problem.
- Offer the cell a forbidden shortcut.

### Corrupt Institution

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Continuity, profit, reputation, order, concealment |
| **Protects Itself With** | Paperwork, hierarchy, law, plausible deniability |
| **Tells** | Missing records, impossible promotions, repeated exemptions |
| **Pushback** | Audits, jurisdictional challenges, formal complaints, delays |

Example moves:

- Demand documentation.
- Escalate to a superior.
- Bury evidence in procedure.
- Offer cooperation that traps the cell.
- Blame a subordinate.
- Turn the cell’s authority into a public issue.

### Noble Conspiracy

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Power, inheritance, revenge, status, protection from scandal |
| **Protects Itself With** | Servants, retainers, duels, patronage, marriages, assassins |
| **Tells** | Quiet payments, sealed rooms, social exclusions, private chapels |
| **Pushback** | Social traps, hired killers, blackmail, rival investigators |

Example moves:

- Invite the cell into a controlled social space.
- Put an innocent servant between the cell and the truth.
- Challenge status rather than facts.
- Weaponize etiquette.
- Offer an alliance against a worse enemy.
- Make exposure politically catastrophic.

### Heretek Network

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Forbidden invention, machine liberation, immortality, secrecy |
| **Protects Itself With** | Scrapcode, servitors, forge law, hidden workshops |
| **Tells** | Machine dreams, wrong serials, impossible repairs, silent servitors |
| **Pushback** | Lockouts, data corruption, weaponized systems, Mechanicus claims |

Example moves:

- Turn the environment into a threat.
- Make evidence machine-readable but not human-readable.
- Offer a technological miracle.
- Claim the cell is interfering with sacred Mechanicus jurisdiction.
- Corrupt a tool the cell depends on.
- Leave a useful device that wants to be used.

### Rogue Psyker / Witch Circle

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Survival, control, recognition, revenge, transcendence |
| **Protects Itself With** | Sympathy, fear, dreams, mental influence, hidden victims |
| **Tells** | Nightmares, nosebleeds, impossible coincidences, miracle rumors |
| **Pushback** | Psychic traces, possessed witnesses, panic, corruption stress |

Example moves:

- Make a witness remember what did not happen.
- Show the psyker as victim and threat.
- Create a miracle rumor.
- Put the Sanctioned Psyker under suspicion.
- Mark someone with a soul impression.
- Let the Warp notice.

### Xenos Infiltration

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Survival, breeding, intelligence, sabotage, extraction, conquest |
| **Protects Itself With** | Mimicry, cult proxies, alien logic, biological signs, void routes |
| **Tells** | Wrong blood, missing shadows, impossible anatomy, pattern errors |
| **Pushback** | Contamination, ambush, misdirection, body horror, alien bargains |

Example moves:

- Reveal that a human collaborator is sincere.
- Make the alien tool useful.
- Make the evidence biologically dangerous.
- Put the Mechanicus and Ordo Xenos at odds.
- Have the enemy learn the cell’s pattern.
- Make containment more dangerous than destruction.

### Daemonic Incursion

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| **Wants** | Manifestation, corruption, worship, despair, breach, bargain |
| **Protects Itself With** | Lies, possession, cults, wards, impossible events |
| **Tells** | Repeated names, impossible geometry, blasphemous miracles, time loss |
| **Pushback** | Corruption stress, possession attempts, memory wounds, reality damage |

Example moves:

- Make a true statement in the daemon’s voice.
- Offer exactly what the cell needs.
- Possess someone too useful to kill.
- Turn evidence into temptation.
- Make the Patron’s orders morally insufficient.
- Force the cell to choose containment or truth.

## 9. Factions

Factions should complicate the mission even when they are not the enemy.

Every faction should have:

- A public face.
- A private agenda.
- A resource the cell wants.
- A reason to resist the cell.
- A line they will not cross.
- A thing they are wrong about.

### Faction Template

Private Agenda:

### Common Imperial Factions

| Faction | Useful For | Dangerous Because |
|---|---|---|
| **Administratum** | Records, identities, tithes, permissions | Slow, suspicious, paper trails |
| **Arbites / Enforcers** | Arrests, cordons, interrogations, law | Jurisdiction, witnesses, escalation |
| **Mechanicus** | Machines, data, augury, containment | Secrecy, rival doctrine, tech claims |
| **Ecclesiarchy** | Faith, crowds, legitimacy, confession | Zeal, public panic, moral pressure |
| **Astra Militarum** | Troops, logistics, firepower | Chain of command, collateral damage |
| **Noble Houses** | Access, money, servants, influence | Scandal, assassins, patronage webs |

| **Void Guilds** | Travel, cargo, docks, manifests | Contracts, quarantine, alien contact |
| **Rival Inquisition** | Expertise, authority, hidden truths | Jurisdiction, ideology, purges |

### Faction Reaction Ladder

| State | Faction Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Helpful** | Provides access, favors, or silence. |
| **Cautious** | Helps only with proof, payment, or leverage. |
| **Suspicious** | Records interactions and limits cooperation. |

| **Active Enemy** | Hunts, frames, attacks, or politically destroys the cell. |

## 10. Clue Design

A clue is not just information.

A clue should do at least one of these:

- Reveal a truth.
- Point to a person.
- Point to a place.
- Reframe a previous scene.
- Create a new danger.
- Implicate a faction.
- Offer a forbidden solution.
- Show what will happen if the cell does nothing.

### Clue Types

| Clue Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Physical** | Corpse, wound, mutation, ritual mark, xenos residue |
| **Documentary** | Ledger, confession, manifest, sealed order, prayer rota |
| **Social** | Witness, rumor, informant, guilty official, frightened servant |
| **Occult** | Warp stain, dream, daemonic name, prophecy, possession scar |
| **Technical** | Cogitator log, auspex trace, pict-feed, scrapcode |
| **Institutional** | Missing tithe, impossible promotion, jurisdiction anomaly |
| **Negative** | Missing corpse, erased record, absent witness, impossible silence |

### Three-Clue Method

For every Core Revelation, prepare at least three ways the cell can learn it.

| Core Revelation | Clue Path |
|---|---|
| The tithe shortfall is deliberate. | Ledger anomaly, missing cargo, dockworker testimony |
| The cult is inside the Ecclesiarchy. | Prayer rota, altered relic inventory, confessor’s fear |
| The corpse was never human. | Autopsy, auspex residue, Mechanicus overreaction |
| The noble is shielding the witch. | Servant gossip, sealed passage, impossible legal exemption |

The cell does not need all three clues. They need enough to act.

### Clue Costs

When the acolytes roll poorly while investigating, use one of these costs:

| Cost | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Late** | They find the clue after the enemy has moved. |
| **Loud** | They find the clue but create Heat. |
| **Partial** | They find the clue but lack context. |
| **Contaminated** | The clue is true but misleading. |
| **Witnessed** | Someone sees them find it. |
| **Dangerous** | The clue harms, corrupts, or tempts them. |
| **Owned** | A faction controls access to the clue. |
| **Perishable** | The clue must be acted on now. |

## 11. Clocks

Clocks track pressure that should build over time.

Use clocks when:

- Trouble should be visible before it lands.
- The cell has a chance to intervene.
- Several failures contribute to one consequence.
- A faction needs time to react.
- The enemy is advancing off-screen.

Do not use a clock when the consequence should happen immediately.

### Clock Sizes

| Clock Size | Use For |
|---:|---|
| **4 segments** | Immediate local threat |
| **6 segments** | Operation-level danger |
| **8 segments** | Mission-level danger |
| **10 segments** | Campaign-level threat |

### Example Clocks

| Clock | Segments | Advances When | When Full |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Cult Moves the Prisoner** | 4 | Heat rises, interrogation fails, time passes | The prisoner vanishes into the ritual site. |
| **Arbites Connect the Pattern** | 4 | Public violence, witnesses, records | Local law intervenes. |
| **Cover Audit** | 4 | Cover is questioned, false papers used | Cover becomes Compromised. |
| **Enemy Identifies the Cell** | 6 | Survivors escape, methods repeat | Awareness rises to 6. |
| **Rival Cell Arrives** | 6 | Patron Notice, Heat thresholds | Rival acolytes enter the Operation. |
| **Ritual Completion** | 8 | Delays, sacrifices, failed containment | The daemon manifests. |
| **Public Panic** | 4 | Psychic phenomena, bodies, rumors | Riot, purge, or mass confession. |
| **Patron Judgment** | 6 | Red Lines, Patron Notice, hidden evidence | The Patron issues correction. |

### Advancing Clocks

Advance a clock by 1 when:

- A relevant roll is 7 or lower.
- The cell delays or lays low.
- The enemy is ignored.
- Heat reaches a threshold.
- A clue is found too late.
- Fallout gives the threat an opening.

Advance a clock by 2 when:

- The cell suffers Severe Fallout.
- Heat reaches 9.
- The enemy captures an asset.
- A Red Line is crossed.
- The cell chooses a major shortcut.

| 4 | Local enforcer who resents outside authority |
| 5 | Tech-adept who knows the machine is lying |
| 6 | Priest who thinks exposure will destroy faith |
| 7 | Noble heir with a useful scandal |
| 8 | Underworld broker who wants immunity |
| 9 | Survivor with corrupted memories |
| 10 | Rival agent pretending to cooperate |

## 13. Running Investigations

Investigations should move through pressure, not locked doors.

### Investigation Loop

1. Present an unstable situation.
2. Let the cell choose an approach.
3. Give clues generously.
4. Attach costs to dangerous clues.
5. Let the enemy react.
6. Escalate Heat, Awareness, or clocks.
7. Ask what truth the cell acts on.
8. Force a judgment.

### Investigation Failure Modes

Avoid:

- “You find nothing.”
- “You missed the only clue.”
- “The mission stalls.”
- “Try another roll.”
- “Nothing changes.”

Use:

- “You find it too late.”
- “You find it, but someone sees.”
- “You find it, but it implicates your ally.”
- “You find it, but it is partly false.”
- “You find it, but touching it marks you.”
- “You find it, but now the enemy knows you know.”

## 14. Running Violence

Combat is a Situation, not a separate game mode. But it is a Situation with special stakes: violence is loud, violent is costly, and violence leaves marks — on the acolytes, on their Cover, on Heat, and on their souls.

### The Principle: Prepare or Suffer

**Unprepared combat will be dangerous and will hurt.**

Acolytes who kick down doors and roll 3D10 at Dangerous are gambling with their bodies, their minds, and their souls. The odds are against them. They will take stress. They will suffer Fallout. They will be changed by it.

This is by design. The game is not about winning fights. It is about making sure that when the fighting starts, you have already won.

**The cell should be trying to reduce combat's difficulty narratively and stack more dice through Mastery and help.** Every scene before the combat is a chance to tilt the odds:

| Preparation | Mechanical Effect |
|---|---|
| Investigate the enemy (find a weakness) | Mastery (+1D10) on relevant rolls |
| Requisition the right gear | Better stress dice, Protection, tags |
| Set up an ambush or positioning | Step down Difficulty (Dangerous -> Risky) |
| Use Cover to get close | Step down Difficulty, gain surprise |
| Coordinate with allies | Help (+1D10 per helper) |
| Exploit a known vulnerability | Reduce enemy Resistance, add tags |

A cell that does none of this rolls 3D10 at Dangerous. They will probably fail, and failure in combat means stress, Fallout, and clocks ticking.

A cell that prepares properly rolls 5D10 at Safe or Risky. They will probably succeed, and success means they control the cost.

**Neither outcome is "wrong."** The unprepared cell gets a desperate, costly fight that leaves scars. The prepared cell gets a controlled, efficient operation. Both are good stories. But the players should understand the difference before they choose.

### When Players Want to Fight

When the cell initiates violence, ask:

1. **What does the cell want?** (Kill, capture, drive off, destroy, contain?)
2. **What does the opposition want?** (Survive, complete the ritual, escape, call for help?)
3. **What makes the violence costly?** (Witnesses, sacred ground, civilians, evidence?)
4. **Who might see it?** (Heat trigger)
5. **What evidence will remain?** (Bodies, shells, picts, Warp Trace?)
6. **What changes if the cell wins loudly?** (Clocks, faction response, Patron Notice?)

Then ask the key question:

7. **Did the cell prepare for this?**

If yes — reward the preparation. Step down Difficulty, grant Mastery, let Cover work. The combat should feel like the payoff for good investigation and planning.

If no — the combat is Dangerous or worse. The cell is improvising. Stress will land. Fallout will trigger. This is the cost of acting without preparation.

### The Combat Prep Procedure

Before any significant combat, briefly walk through:

```
GM: "The cult is in the candle-vault. You've tracked them here.
     What do you do before you breach?"

Players might:
- Send Lys in disguised (Cover step-down)
- Requisition warded gear (gear advantage)
- Review what they know about the cult (clue -> Mastery)
- Coordinate with Arbites (help, but Heat)
- Set up a chokepoint (positioning -> step-down)

Each "yes" improves the odds.
No prep = Dangerous with 3D10. Full prep = Safe with 5D10+.
```

This should take 2-3 minutes, not 20. It's a quick checklist, not a separate scene. The point is to make the players consciously invest in preparation, then reward that investment when the dice hit the table.

### Designing Enemies That Reward Preparation

Enemies should have **discoverable weaknesses** that the cell can find through investigation. These weaknesses should meaningfully change the combat math.

| Weakness Type | How to Discover | Combat Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Coward** | Interrogation, witness testimony | Resistance reduced by 2 if threatened directly |
| **Vulnerable to wards** | Occult research, Mechanicus consult | Warded weapons inflict double stress |
| **Dependent on a ritual component** | Investigating the rite | Destroying the component halves Resistance |
| **Bound to a location** | Tracing the warp signature | Luring them away reduces their Protection |
| **Has a handler** | Following the command chain | Capturing the handler forces surrender |
| **Marked by a previous cell** | Finding old case files | Mastery on all rolls against them |

Without discovering the weakness, the enemy is at full strength. With the weakness, the fight becomes manageable. This makes investigation mechanically meaningful — it's not just flavor, it's the difference between a desperate brawl and a controlled takedown.

### Ambushes and Survival Fights

Sometimes the cell doesn't get to prepare. The cult strikes first. A daemon manifests. The Arbites raid the safehouse. The cell is caught in the open.

**This is good.** Unprepared combat should feel desperate. The cell should be fighting to survive, to escape, to hold on long enough to turn the tables. These are the moments that define acolytes -- not the clean operations, but the times everything went wrong and they endured.

**When does an ambush happen?** Ambushes are triggered by the **Conditional Ambush** rules tied to Heat Moves (see Heat and Subtlety, section 5). The GM doesn't decide arbitrarily -- the fiction and the Heat level determine when the enemy strikes:

- **Heat 3 (Rumors):** No ambush. The enemy is watching but doesn't know enough to strike. Heat Moves at this tier are about scrutiny and pressure, not violence.
- **Heat 6 (Hunted):** On enemy-action Heat Moves, check if the cell is in a vulnerable position. If yes, the enemy strikes -- Dangerous, no prep bonuses on the first round.
- **Heat 9 (Burned):** Ambushes escalate. At Enemy Awareness 4+, most enemy moves trigger automatic ambushes. At 6+, the enemy knows where the cell will be -- Impossible difficulty.

The key question is always: **"Is the cell in a vulnerable position right now?"** If they're traveling, meeting contacts, entering compromised locations, or approaching watched sites, the ambush triggers. If they're in a secure position or have set their own counter-ambush, they react normally.

**Ambushes start at Hunted.** At Rumors, the enemy is watching but doesn't know enough to set an effective ambush. At Hunted, they're acting -- and if the cell is in a vulnerable position when an enemy-action move triggers, the ambush fires. At Burned with high Awareness, ambushes are nearly unavoidable.

When the cell is ambushed:
- Set Difficulty at Dangerous or Impossible
- Let the first round be about survival, not victory
- Give them a way out -- a clock to fill, a position to reach, a ritual to disrupt
- Make the cost real: stress, Fallout, burned Cover, ticking clocks
- Let them survive, but changed

**Fighting a daemon should mean you are changed by the Fallout.** Corruption is not abstract. It is the warp touching your soul. A cell that fights a daemon and walks away clean hasn't understood what they survived. The Fallout is the story.

### Violence Outcomes

When violence occurs, consider:

| Question | Possible Consequence |
|---|---|
| Were weapons loud? | Heat +1 |
| Were there witnesses? | Heat +1 or clock tick |
| Did anyone escape? | Enemy Awareness +1 |
| Were restricted weapons used? | Authority or Shadow stress |
| Were civilians harmed? | Mind or Authority stress |
| Was the target important? | Faction response |
| Was the scene secured? | Evidence remains or vanishes |
| Did the violence solve the wrong problem? | New clue or new threat |

### Harm and Defeat

When an NPC or threat takes stress equal to its Resistance, choose what defeat means:

- Killed.
- Captured.
- Exposed.
- Routed.
- Silenced.
- Discredited.
- Banished.
- Contained.
- Forced to bargain.
- Made useful.

Defeat does not have to mean death.

### Calibrated Combat Examples

| Situation | Likely Pool | Difficulty | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Secutor ambushes cult guards with Mastery, help, and positioning | 5D10 | Safe or Risky | Full prep. The ambush is the payoff. |
| Legate kicks down the door with no intel, no backup | 3D10 | Dangerous | Unprepared. The Legate is gambling. |
| Cell assaults a daemonhost after requisitioning warded chains and researching its true name | 5D10 | Risky | Good prep but the daemon is still deadly. |
| Penumbra is ambushed by cult assassins while alone | 3D10 | Dangerous or Impossible | No prep, no help. Survival, not victory. |
| Cell raids the candle-vault after Lys infiltrated and mapped it | 4D10 | Risky | Partial prep. Cover gave them the layout. |
| Psyker confronts a warp manifestation with no wards, no preparation | 3D10 | Impossible | The warp does not care about your courage. |

Social scenes should have stakes.

1. What does the cell want?
2. What does the NPC want?

4. What can the cell lose?

## 16. Running Forbidden Methods

Forbidden Methods should be tempting because they work.

They should be dangerous because they leave a mark.

When a player proposes a Forbidden Method, ask:

1. Why is this faster than the loyal method?
2. What does it require?
3. Who would condemn it?
4. What kind of evidence does it leave?
5. What does it want afterward?
6. What would the Patron say publicly?
7. What would the Patron say privately?

### Forbidden Method Costs

| Cost | Example |
|---|---|
| **Corruption stress** | Warp mark, xenos infection, daemonic whisper |
| **Shadow stress** | Hidden evidence, witness, forbidden trace |
| **Authority stress** | Puritan suspicion, faction complaint |
| **Mind stress** | Guilt, obsession, memory scar |
| **Heat** | Someone sees or records the method |
| **Awareness** | The enemy recognizes the method |
| **Clock** | The method creates future hunger or exposure |
| **Debt** | The tool, entity, or informant wants payment |

For full procedure, result bands, Forbidden Assets, Warp Trace, and refresh guidance, use [Psychic Powers and Forbidden Methods v0.1](10-psychic-powers-and-forbidden-methods-v0.1.md). At the table, pick the pressure that best expresses the cost instead of adding every cost at once.

## 17. Running Patron Pressure

The Patron should be felt even when absent.

Use Patron pressure when:

- The cell delays.
- Heat rises.
- A Red Line is approached.
- A Boon is used loudly.
- The cell hides evidence.
- A Radical method succeeds.
- A Humanity choice conflicts with orders.
- Another faction complains.
- Severe Fallout changes the Mission.

### Patron Moves

| Move | Use When |
|---|---|
| **Demand Explanation** | The cell caused noise, delay, or contradiction. |
| **Offer a Terrible Resource** | The cell is stuck or desperate. |
| **Narrow the Mandate** | The cell is wandering or hesitating. |
| **Reveal Hidden Knowledge** | The Patron knew more than they said. |
| **Threaten Disavowal** | The cell becomes politically inconvenient. |
| **Punish Mercy** | The cell preserved something the Patron wanted destroyed. |
| **Reward Ruthlessness** | The cell crossed a line the Patron secretly approves. |
| **Trigger Liability** | The Patron’s weakness enters play. |
| **Spend Patience** | The Patron tolerates a mistake but marks it. |
| **Issue Judgment** | The cell has become part of the problem. |

### Patron Communication

Patron messages should be short.

Examples:

- “Explain.”
- “Proceed.”
- “No witnesses.”
- “Preserve the artifact.”
- “Destroy the artifact.”
- “You were not authorized.”
- “I expected worse.”
- “Do not make me visible.”
- “Bring me the witch alive.”
- “You misunderstand mercy.”

## 19. Operation Prep Procedure

Use this when preparing a session.

### Step 1: Define the Operation

```text
Operation Name:
Objective:
Starting Heat:
Starting Enemy Awareness:
Cover Story:
Primary Opposition:
Core Revelation:
Exit Condition:
```

### Step 2: Build the Opposition

Give the opposition:

- Objective.
- Instinct.
- Tell.
- Resistance.
- Stress Die.
- Protection.
- Heat 3 / 6 / 9 responses.

### Step 3: Build the Clue Web

Create:

- 1 Core Revelation.
- 3 ways to discover it.
- 1 false lead.
- 1 dangerous truth.
- 1 person who lies.
- 1 person who knows but is afraid.

### Step 4: Add Factions

Choose 2–3 factions.

For each, define:

- What they want.
- What they can provide.
- What they will hide.
- What makes them hostile.

### Step 5: Add Clocks

Create 2–4 clocks:

- Enemy plan.
- Local authority.
- Cover audit.
- Public panic.
- Patron judgment.
- Rival arrival.
- Evidence review.

### Step 6: Add Fallout Prompts

Prepare one fallout idea for each Resistance:

| Resistance | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Body** | Injury, poison, exhaustion, infection, radiation |
| **Mind** | Fear, guilt, obsession, memory scar |
| **Shadow** | Witness, pict capture, damaged Cover |
| **Authority** | Complaint, audit, superior, jurisdiction |
| **Corruption** | Warp Trace, xenos mark, forbidden appetite |

### Step 7: Add a Forbidden Shortcut

Every good Operation should have one terrible efficient answer.

Examples:

- Torture the witness.
- Use xenotech to decode the signal.
- Let the daemon speak through the corpse.
- Frame a rival faction.
- Flash the Rosette publicly.
- Purge the whole hab-block.
- Trade with the cult splinter.
- Destroy the evidence to prevent panic.

## 20. Session Start Procedure

At the start of play:

1. Recap the Mandate.
2. Recap current Heat and Awareness.
3. Recap active clocks.
4. Ask each player for Duty, Humanity, and Radical Beats.
5. Ask what Cover the cell is using.
6. Ask what the cell wants to accomplish first.
7. Start in motion.

Start with pressure already moving.

Examples:

- The witness is being transferred now.
- The corpse is already on the slab.
- The noble gala begins in one hour.
- The cult courier boards the lift.
- The Arbites cordon is forming.
- The Patron has sent a one-word order.

At the end of a session:

1. Resolve completed Beats.
2. Award Advances or Requisitions.
3. Review Heat.
4. Review Enemy Awareness.

7. Ask what evidence remains.

## 22. One-Page GM Reference

### GM Load-Bearing Checklist

Use this when a Situation starts to wobble and you need to make a fast, fair call.

| Question | Table Answer |
|---|---|
| Roll or no roll? | Roll only if failure or cost changes the situation. |
| What is at stake? | Name the thing at risk before the dice hit the table. |
| Difficulty? | Set the chance of each result band: Safe, Risky, Dangerous, or Impossible. |
| Resistance? | Choose Body, Mind, Shadow, Authority, or Corruption. |
| Stress die? | Set cost severity: D3, D6, D8, or D10. |
| Heat or Awareness? | Heat if the Operation gets noisier; Awareness if the enemy learns something specific. |
| Clock? | Tick a clock when the consequence should build instead of landing immediately. |
| Fallout prompt? | Know what concrete consequence could appear if stress spills over. |

### During Play

When unsure, ask:

1. What is at stake?
2. What Resistance is risked?
3. Who notices?
4. What evidence remains?
5. What does the enemy learn?
6. What would the Patron think?
7. What clock advances?

### On 7 or Lower

Choose one or more:

- Inflict stress.
- Raise Heat.
- Raise Awareness.
- Damage Cover.
- Tick a clock.
- Reveal a cost.
- Split the cell.
- Put an NPC at risk.
- Trigger a faction response.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- Offer a worse bargain.
- Reveal a dangerous truth.

### When the Cell Succeeds

Ask:

- What changes because they succeeded?
- Who is now afraid?
- Who is now exposed?
- What new option opens?
- What new danger becomes visible?

Success should move the game forward.

Clean success means they avoid the immediate cost.

It does not mean the world stops reacting.

## 23. Design Notes

This toolkit assumes the GM is not hiding a single correct path.

The cell should have many ways forward:

- Covert investigation.
- Social pressure.
- Official authority.
- Violence.
- Requisition.
- Forbidden methods.
- Patron intervention.
- Faction leverage.
- Sacrifice.

The game becomes interesting when every route works and every route costs something different.

The best question in this game is not:

**Can the acolytes win?**

They probably can.

The better question is:

**What will still be true after they do?**
