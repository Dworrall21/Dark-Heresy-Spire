## Character Questions

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| **Why did the Inquisition choose you?** | You survived a voidship warp breach as a child and emerged a psyker. The Black Ship took you, but instead of the pyre or the Golden Throne, you were sent to the Schola Progenium on Trinamor VI — sorted, disciplined, and pointed at the Emperor's enemies. You never asked for this. You just wanted to get out alive. |
| **What makes you useful?** | You are a sanctioned psyker with training from both the Black Ship and the Schola. You can read minds, move objects with will alone, tear open warp portals, and peer into the immaterial. You are the cell's occult sensor, heavy support, and emergency escape route. And when channeled, you fight. |
| **What makes you dangerous to keep alive?** | You are emotionally callous, reckless, and vengeful. You'll use forbidden methods — Obscura (opioids), stim-injections, and worse — to survive. Your relationship with the Warp is closer than most; the Gellar breach left its mark. You suffer Despair in social situations (hard Fear checks), and your Death Sight makes you unsettling to be around. You have almost no filter: long conversations and eye contact are your enemies. |

## Translation Notes

**Character Summary:**

Tacitus is a **Sanctioned Psyker** translated from a high-power Genesys Dark Heresy character. He is a Schola Progenium graduate with a Black Ship past, rogue trader origins, and a Gellar breach survival that left him warp-touched beyond normal sanctioning. He is the cell's psychic heavy support, occult sensor, and emergency escape route (Gate of Infinity).

**Key Mapping Decisions:**

- **Int 4, Willpower 3, Discipline 2 → Mind Protection 2.** Sanctioning + Schola conditioning. His mental fortification is real but tested.
- **Brawn 2, Agility 2, Int 4 → No melee Mastery.** He's not a fighter physically. His combat power is entirely psychic.
- **Psy Rating 4 (Genesys) → 5 Psychic Powers + multiple Major Advances.** His Genesys psychic talent investment is very high. This maps to an above-average number of powers and significant psychic-focused advances.
- **Favored By the Warp → Roll Perils twice, pick result.** This is a massive safety net. In Spire, this is a Major Advance that makes psychic use significantly safer — but the GM should make the "better" result still interesting.
- **Corpus Conversion → Pay 2 Wounds for +1 success on psychic checks.** Desperation fuel. This is a Major Advance that lets Tacitus trade his body for warp power.
- **Psychic Mastery → Double-cast 1/Encounter.** His signature combat trick. Two psychic powers in one turn, once per encounter.
- **Death Sight + Despair → Passive warp-touched abilities.** These are not standard class powers. They're Forbidden Edge / warp exposure consequences that give him offensive and social-disruption abilities at the cost of Corruption stress and social isolation.
- **Stims + Obscura → Chemical dependency package.** Two expendable resources that give short-term power at long-term cost. Dependency clocks are a real threat.
- **Cranial-Temporal Implant → Experimental cybernetics.** Grants Mastery on data tasks but risks Mechanicus hostility. May have warp-resonant properties.
- **Melua? → Unknown item.** Deliberately left as a mystery for the GM to develop. Tied to the Peradventure.
- **Talents → Advances.** 20+ Genesys talents collapse into 4 Minor + 6 Major + 2 Severe advances. This is a high-advancement character — appropriate for a high-power Genesys build.

**What makes him powerful:**
- 5 Psychic Powers including Gate of Infinity (warp portal escape)
- Favored By the Warp (Perils safety net)
- Psychic Mastery (double-cast)
- Corpus Conversion (wounds-for-power)
- Push 4 (extended psychic output)
- Rosarius (Corruption Protection + Fear aura for allies)
- Runic Staff (melee + psy-focus combined)
- Death Sight (offensive Fear attack)
- Multiple strain recovery abilities (Rapid Recovery, Second Wind, Desperate Recovery)

**What keeps him balanced:**
- Corruption stress starts at 1 (the Gellar breach left its mark)
- Shadow Protection 0 (he's visibly a psyker with a brand and cranial implant)
- Despair aura makes social interaction nearly impossible
- Chemical dependency risk (stims + Obscura)
- Cranial implant may be heretek tech
- Melua? is an unknown warp-tainted item
- Peradventure Echo resource ticks Warp Trace — at 6/6, something finds him
- Authority Protection only 1 (limited institutional backing)
- Body Protection only 2 (light armor, no physical toughness)
- No melee Mastery, no ranged Mastery (his combat is entirely psychic)
- Emotionally callous and reckless — the cell has to manage him
