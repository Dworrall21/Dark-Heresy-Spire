# Mission and Operation Template v0.1

This document turns the campaign scale into a repeatable prep format.

The game is structured as:

**Campaign -> Mission -> Operation -> Situation**

| Scale | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Campaign** | The larger conspiracy, sector crisis, or Patron agenda. |
| **Mission** | The cell's current major assignment. |
| **Operation** | A discrete objective within the Mission. |
| **Situation** | A scene where something is at stake. |

---

## 1. Campaign Sheet

Use this for the long-running conspiracy or sector-level arc.

```text
Campaign Name:
Central Heresy:
Primary Threat Type:
Patron:
Rival:
Sector / Subsector / Theater:
Opening Assumption:
Hidden Truth:
What Happens If No One Intervenes:
```

### Campaign Questions

1. What does the Patron think is happening?
2. What is actually happening?
3. Which faction benefits from the cover story?
4. Which faction would rather the truth remain buried?
5. What makes this too dangerous for open Imperial force?
6. What forbidden solution would solve the problem quickly?
7. What would that forbidden solution cost?

### Campaign Clocks

Use 2-4 campaign clocks. They should move when Missions fail, when Heat reaches 9, when the cell delays, or when the enemy is ignored.

| Clock | Segments | What Advances It | What Happens When Full |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Enemy Master Plan | 6 / 8 / 10 | Failed Operations, escaped suspects, ignored clues | The conspiracy changes scale. |
| Patron Suspicion | 4 / 6 | Radical Beats, hidden evidence, Red Line breaches | The Patron audits or threatens the cell. |
| Rival Agenda | 4 / 6 | Public action, political scandal, faction complaints | A rival cell or faction acts. |
| Public Crisis | 4 / 6 / 8 | Heat, riots, miracles, plague, purges | The situation becomes impossible to hide. |

## 2. Mission Sheet

A Mission is the cell's current major assignment.

```text
Mission Name:
Patron Brief:
Official Cover:
Real Suspicion:
Primary Location:
Required Subtlety:
Mandate Objective:
Forbidden Temptation:
Mission Failure State:
```

### Mission Elements

| Element | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | What does the Patron want accomplished? |
| **Cover Story** | Why is the cell officially there? |
| **Hidden Truth** | What is really going on? |
| **Opening Hook** | What forces the cell to act now? |
| **Key Factions** | Who wants, hides, fears, or profits from the truth? |
| **Primary Threat** | Cult, daemon, xenos, heretek, noble, psyker, rival, plague, etc. |
| **Red Lines** | What must not happen during this Mission? |
| **Resources** | What can the Patron provide? |
| **Forbidden Offer** | What terrible solution is available? |
| **End State** | What counts as success, partial success, and failure? |

## 3. Operation Sheet

An Operation is a manageable, discrete goal inside a Mission.

```text
Operation Name:
Objective:
Starting Heat:
Starting Enemy Awareness:
Primary Opposition:
Key Location:
Core Clue:
Complication:
Exit Condition:
```

### Operation Checklist

Every Operation should include:

- Objective
- Opposition
- Starting Heat
- Enemy Awareness
- At least one Core Clue
- At least two optional clues
- One environmental pressure
- One faction pressure
- One possible Fallout prompt
- One Requisition opportunity
- One enemy reaction if ignored
- One way to complete it quietly
- One way to complete it loudly
- One way it can go terribly wrong

---

## 4. Core Clue Rule

Never hide the core clue.

If a clue is necessary for the mission to move forward, a failed roll should not mean “you find nothing.” The roll determines what it costs to get the clue.

| Roll | Investigation Outcome |
|---|---|
| 1 | You find the clue, but trigger a major complication, false assumption, or enemy response. |
| 2-5 | You find the clue, but too late, too loudly, or with missing context. |
| 6-7 | You find the clue, but take stress. |
| 8-9 | You find the clue cleanly. |
| 10 | You find the clue and an extra lead, weakness, or opportunity. |

```text
Likely Rolls:

```

### Situation Questions

1. What can the acolytes gain here?
2. What can they lose here?
3. What changes if they do nothing?
4. Who might witness the scene?
5. What evidence might remain afterward?
6. What would make this Risky or Dangerous?
7. What forbidden method would solve it immediately?
8. What would that forbidden method cost?

### Threat Traits

| Trait | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Difficulty** | Safe, Risky, Dangerous, or Impossible to act against. |
| **Resistance** | Stress needed to defeat, expose, capture, silence, or banish. |
| **Stress Die** | Stress inflicted when the threat pushes back. |
| **Protection** | Stress ignored or absorbed. |
| **Instinct** | What the threat naturally does when not stopped. |
| **Tell** | What reveals its nature. |
| **Clue / Resource** | What the cell can gain from it. |

### Clue Types

| Clue Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Physical** | Corpse, mutation, ritual mark, xenos residue, weapon burn. |
| **Documentary** | Ledger, prayer rota, shipping manifest, sealed confession. |
| **Social** | Witness, rumor, informant, guilty official, frightened servant. |
| **Occult** | Warp stain, dream, possession scar, daemonic name, prophetic echo. |
| **Technical** | Cogitator logs, pict-feed, scrapcode, auspex trace, machine-spirit complaint. |
| **Institutional** | Missing tithe, falsified order, impossible promotion, sealed archive gap. |

## 8. Operation Heat Integration

Each Operation starts with a Heat rating.

| Starting Heat | Use When |
|---:|---|
| 0 | The cell is completely covert. |
| 1-2 | The cell has a plausible cover, but the area is tense. |
| 3-4 | Someone already suspects interference. |
| 5-6 | The cell enters an active crisis or watched location. |
| 7+ | The Operation begins nearly burned. |

When Heat reaches 3, 6, or 9, make a Heat Move from [Heat and Subtlety v0.1](06-heat-subtlety-v0.1-outline.md).

## 9. Requisition Opportunities

Every Operation should include at least one chance to gain or spend resources.

| Opportunity | Example |
|---|---|
| **Local Contact** | A frightened clerk, underhive guide, shrine keeper, dock factor. |
| **Imperial Asset** | Arbites cordon, medicae access, Munitorum pass, Mechanicus scanner. |
| **Forbidden Asset** | Cult token, xenos shard, daemonological scrap, rogue psyker rumor. |
| **Cover Asset** | New identity, official uniform, falsified work order, noble invitation. |
| **Emergency Asset** | Extraction route, safehouse, weapons cache, cleanup crew. |

## 10. Fallout Prompts by Operation Type

| Operation Type | Fallout Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Infiltration** | Cover Identity compromised; safehouse watched; witness notices inconsistency. |
| **Interrogation** | False confession; subject dies; handler notices psychic or physical trace. |
| **Surveillance** | Pict evidence cuts both ways; the observer is observed. |
| **Raid** | Evidence destroyed; collateral damage; Heat spike; enemy escapes. |
| **Occult Containment** | Corruption mark; possession attempt; ward traps something inside. |
| **Xenos Investigation** | Alien logic infection; Mechanicus interest; artifact teaches the acolyte. |
| **Political Maneuver** | Factional complaint; rival acolyte interest; Patron Red Line approached. |
| **Humanitarian Crisis** | Mercy risks mission; purge solves problem too well; civilians become witnesses. |

## 11. Mission End Procedure

At the end of a Mission, answer:

1. Was the Mandate fulfilled?
2. What truth was revealed?
3. What truth was hidden?
4. What evidence was preserved, destroyed, or falsified?
5. Who knows the cell was involved?
6. Which Bonds, Covers, Boons, or Resources changed?
7. Did the cell cross a Red Line?
8. What does the Patron think happened?
9. What actually happened?
10. What enemy, rival, or consequence follows them forward?

Then perform:

- Patron Debrief
- Beat rewards
- End-of-Mission Change Awards if appropriate
- Boon refresh or step-down
- Heat reset or conversion into campaign consequence
- Enemy clock updates

## 12. Example Mission Frame

**Campaign:** The Ashen Tithe Heresy  
**Mission:** Discover why Hive Carcosa's tithe has collapsed.  
**Official Cover:** Administratum tithe auditors.  
**Real Suspicion:** Heretical infiltration of labor and noble supply chains.  
**Forbidden Temptation:** Plant evidence against a minor noble house to flush out the true patron.

**Operation 1:** Embed among Administratum auditors.  
**Operation 2:** Trace missing shipments through underhive routes.  
**Operation 3:** Identify the noble house shielding the cult.  
**Operation 4:** Decide whether to purge, expose, or exploit the truth.
