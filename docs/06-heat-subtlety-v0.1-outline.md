# Heat and Subtlety v0.1

This section turns **cover, witnesses, Rosette use, Shadow fallout, forbidden methods, and mission exposure** into a table-facing Operation procedure.

Heat is not a punishment for doing interesting things. Heat is the cost of leaving marks in a paranoid, hierarchical, surveilled Imperium.

Acolytes can win at high Heat. They just cannot win quietly.

---

## 1. Core Distinction: Shadow, Heat, and Enemy Awareness

This system tracks three related but separate pressures.

| Track | Scale | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Shadow Stress** | Individual acolyte / sometimes cell | Your secrecy, aliases, cover identity, surveillance risk, evidence trail, and personal exposure. |
| **Heat** | Operation | How visible, noisy, and unstable the current Operation has become. |
| **Enemy Awareness** | Primary opposition | What the main enemy specifically knows about the cell and its methods. |

### Shadow

Shadow is a Resistance. It lives on character sheets.

You mark Shadow stress when your acolyte's personal secrecy, cover, alias, method, or evidence trail is threatened.

Shadow fallout might mean:

- Your alias is compromised.
- Your face is captured on pict-feed.
- Your safehouse is watched.
- A witness remembers your voice.
- The enemy learns your true name.
- Your cover identity no longer explains your presence.

### Heat

Heat is shared by the whole Operation.

It rises when the cell leaves public marks, creates witnesses, invokes authority, uses loud violence, reveals psychic phenomena, burns cover, or lets evidence spread.

Heat changes the operational environment.

### Enemy Awareness

Enemy Awareness tracks what the primary opposition knows.

Heat can rise without Enemy Awareness rising. A loud brawl can bring local law down on the area without telling the cult who the acolytes are.

Enemy Awareness can rise without Heat rising. One escaped cult courier can tell the cult everything while the public remains ignorant.

## 2. Heat Scale

Each Operation has a shared Heat rating from **0 to 9**.

| Heat | State | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| **0-2** | **Ghosts** | The cell is unseen, dismissed, or hidden beneath a plausible fiction. The cell chooses when to act. |
| **3-5** | **Rumors** | Someone knows something is wrong. Questions begin. Cover still works, but it is being tested. |
| **6-8** | **Hunted** | Enemies, authorities, rivals, or witnesses are actively moving. The Operation has become contested. |
| **9+** | **Burned** | The Operation changes violently, collapses, or becomes public. Quiet completion is no longer possible without major sacrifice. |

Heat can rise above 9, but the track does not need more numbers. Once Heat is Burned, additional Heat should trigger harsher Heat Moves, Patron pressure, faction response, or Operation clocks.

## 3. Starting Heat

Set starting Heat at the beginning of each Operation.

| Starting Heat | Use When |
|---:|---|
| **0** | The cell is completely covert, remote, or unknown. |
| **1** | The area is normal, but the cell's cover is imperfect. |
| **2** | The cell enters a tense or monitored location with a plausible cover. |
| **3** | Someone already suspects interference, crime, heresy, or outsiders. |
| **4** | The Operation begins under active scrutiny or in a politically sensitive zone. |
| **5** | The cell enters an active crisis, purge, riot, lockdown, or watched investigation. |
| **6** | Opposition is already hunting for infiltrators or saboteurs. |
| **7+** | The Operation begins nearly burned. The cell is arriving late, exposed, or after a prior disaster. |

### Starting Heat Modifiers

Add **+1 starting Heat** if:

- The Patron demands speed over subtlety.
- The cell has recently burned a Cover in the same theater.
- A rival faction is already investigating.
- The target location is under surveillance.
- The enemy has been warned.
- The mission involves psykers, daemons, xenos artifacts, or noble politics.
- The cell is entering a holy site, noble estate, forge facility, voidport, or Arbites-controlled zone without ideal paperwork.

Reduce starting Heat by **1**, minimum 0, if:

- The Patron provides a strong Cover Story.
- The cell spends a Requisition on preparation.
- The cell lays groundwork through Contacts or Bonds.
- The Operation happens in a place where violence, disappearances, or bureaucracy are already routine.
- The cell enters through a trusted local faction.
- The enemy is distracted by another crisis.

## 4. When Heat Rises

Increase Heat when the cell leaves a mark that others can follow.

| Trigger | Heat Increase |
|---|---:|
| Public violence | +1 |
| Loud weapons in a populated, sacred, noble, or monitored place | +1 |
| Heavy weapons, explosives, promethium, or mass casualties | +2 |
| Witnesses survive and can talk | +1 |
| Important witnesses survive and can identify the cell | +2 |
| Pict, audio, biometric, augur, or machine-spirit evidence is left behind | +1 |
| Evidence directly connects the cell to Inquisition methods | +2 |
| The Rosette is revealed privately | +1 |
| The Rosette is revealed publicly | +2 |
| The Patron's name is spoken where it can travel | +1 or +2 |
| Psychic phenomena are witnessed | +1 |
| Obvious daemonic, xenos, or heretek phenomena occur | +1 or +2 |
| A Forbidden Method is used carelessly or publicly | +1 |
| A daemonological, xenos, or heretek asset is exposed | +2 |
| A Cover Identity is burned | +1 |
| A Cover Identity is exposed as false | +1 or +2 |
| Shadow fallout names a witness, evidence trail, or compromised alias | +1 |
| Local authorities are dragged into the Operation | +1 |
| Arbites, Mechanicus, Ecclesiarchy, Militarum, or noble forces intervene openly | +1 or +2 |
| A suspect escapes with useful information | +1 |
| Bodies, prisoners, artifacts, or forbidden evidence are unsecured | +1 |
| A Patron Boon is used dramatically | +1 |
| A rival faction can connect the cell to the event | +1 |
| A Red Line is approached or crossed | +1 or +2 |

If multiple triggers happen in the same Situation, the GM may either add them normally or summarize the whole mess as **+2 Heat** and one Heat Move.

Do not nickel-and-dime every tiny exposure. Heat should rise when the fiction changes.

## 5. Heat Thresholds

When Heat reaches **3, 6, or 9** for the first time in an Operation, the GM makes a Heat Move.

Mark **Patron Notice** when Heat reaches 3, 6, or 9 if any of these are true:

- The Patron cares about discretion.
- The cell used Patron authority.
- The current Operation was supposed to remain covert.
- The rise in Heat involved a Red Line.
- The rise in Heat exposed a Patron Boon, Cover Story, or asset.

### Heat 3: Rumors

Someone notices the pattern.

Choose or roll:

| D10 | Heat Move |
|---:|---|
| 1 | A witness talks to the wrong person. |
| 2 | A local official asks for paperwork, proof, or explanation. |
| 3 | The enemy notices interference and tightens security. |
| 4 | A faction becomes curious about the cell's Cover Identity. |
| 5 | A Contact asks whether the cell is bringing danger to their door. |
| 6 | A clue becomes time-sensitive because someone else is now looking for it. |
| 7 | Pict-feed, cogitator logs, or vox chatter must be altered before review. |
| 8 | A harmless NPC starts connecting details they should not understand. |
| 9 | A rival investigator, journalist, confessor, guild factor, or savant takes interest. |
| 10 | A Patron Liability twitches: someone asks why the cell is making noise. |

At Rumors, the cell is not exposed yet. The pressure is **scrutiny**.

### Heat 6: Hunted

Someone acts against the cell.

Choose or roll:

| D10 | Heat Move |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Local law begins looking for the cell, their aliases, or their methods. |
| 2 | The enemy accelerates their timetable. |
| 3 | A rival acolyte cell, faction agent, or hostile investigator appears. |
| 4 | A safehouse, dead drop, Bond, or Contact is watched. |
| 5 | The enemy plants a false victim, false clue, or counter-infiltrator. |
| 6 | The Patron demands an explanation, proof, or cleaner results. |
| 7 | A Cover Identity now requires active maintenance to keep using. |
| 8 | An enemy names one of the cell's methods and prepares against it. |
| 9 | A faction closes doors: Requisitions from them become Risky or Contested. |
| 10 | The next quiet approach becomes Dangerous unless the cell changes tactics. |

At Hunted, the cell is still functional, but no longer controls the tempo.

### Heat 9: Burned

The Operation cannot continue in the same mode.

Choose or roll:

| D10 | Heat Move |
|---:|---|
| 1 | The enemy knows the cell is Inquisition or Inquisition-adjacent. |
| 2 | The Operation's quiet objective becomes impossible. Continue loud, retreat, or change objectives. |
| 3 | Planetary authorities, nobles, Arbites, Mechanicus, Ecclesiarchy, or military forces intervene. |
| 4 | The cell's current Cover Identities are no longer safe. |
| 5 | The Patron threatens disavowal, recall, punishment, or replacement. |
| 6 | The enemy strikes first: ambush, assassination, hostage-taking, evidence purge, or ritual acceleration. |
| 7 | The enemy destroys, moves, or corrupts the Core Clue. It is still obtainable, but only at major cost. |
| 8 | The cell must sacrifice a Bond, Contact, Cover, Resource, or Boon to keep operating. |
| 9 | A public story forms. It is wrong, dangerous, and politically useful to someone. |
| 10 | The Operation becomes a crisis: purge, flee, expose, negotiate, sacrifice, or finish the job loud. |

At Burned, the question is no longer “Can we stay hidden?”

The question is:

**What do we save before the whole Operation collapses?**

## 6. Heat Moves by Source

Sometimes it is useful to choose a move based on what caused the Heat.

### If Heat Rose Because of Violence

Choose one:

- Reinforcements arrive.
- Witnesses flee in all directions.
- Evidence is destroyed by gunfire, fire, or panic.
- A civilian, Contact, or Bond is injured.
- The target now knows the cell is willing to kill.
- The next social approach is Risky or Dangerous.
- Local authorities lock down exits.
- A weapon trace points toward restricted or Inquisitorial equipment.

### If Heat Rose Because of Authority

Choose one:

- Someone demands to see the Rosette.
- A local official obeys while sending a warning up the chain.
- A rival faction contests jurisdiction.
- The Patron hears about the invocation before the cell reports it.
- A fake acolyte, impostor, or opportunist exploits the rumor.
- The next Requisition is Contested.
- A senior official begins documenting everything.
- The cell is asked to produce prisoners, evidence, or a legal explanation.

### If Heat Rose Because of Psychic Power

Choose one:

- A psyker, witch-finder, daemon, or astropath notices the disturbance.
- Witnesses remember the phenomenon incorrectly but intensely.
- A ward, machine-spirit, or relic reacts.
- The target gains a psychic impression of the psyker.
- Corruption fallout leaves physical or metaphysical evidence.
- The next Warp-related roll is Risky.
- The local faith community interprets the event as miracle or witchcraft.
- A nearby vulnerable mind becomes a vector for further trouble.

### If Heat Rose Because of Forbidden Tools

Choose one:

- An ally realizes the cell crossed a line.
- A Puritan authority gains evidence.
- The tool wants to be used again.
- The method creates a new clue and a new accusation.
- The enemy recognizes the tool as similar to their own.
- The Patron's Secret or Hidden Philosophy becomes harder to conceal.
- A rival Inquisitor gains leverage.
- The tool leaves a signature that can be tracked later.

### If Heat Rose Because of Cover Failure

Choose one:

- An alias is checked against records.
- A witness remembers the wrong detail perfectly.
- The Cover remains usable but now has a handler, auditor, or chaperone.
- Another faction tries to recruit, blackmail, or expose the false identity.
- The cell must choose: repair the Cover, burn it, or redirect suspicion.
- A forged document gets sent to a real office for confirmation.
- A real person with the same role or identity appears.
- The cover works too well and creates new obligations.

## 7. Enemy Awareness Ladder

Heat tells you how exposed the Operation is. Enemy Awareness tells you what the primary opposition specifically understands.

| Awareness | What They Know | Typical Enemy Response |
|---:|---|---|
| **0** | Nothing. The cell is invisible. | Continue existing plan. |
| **1** | Something is wrong. | Increase caution, secure obvious vulnerabilities. |
| **2** | Someone is investigating. | Watch suspects, move evidence, test loyalties. |
| **3** | A covert cell is active. | Set traps, plant false clues, compartmentalize. |
| **4** | The Inquisition may be involved. | Accelerate plan, contact patrons, kill witnesses. |
| **5** | They know the cell's methods. | Counter those methods; prepare specific defenses. |
| **6** | They know names, faces, aliases, gene-signs, or soul-impressions. | Strike at Bonds, Covers, safehouses, and extraction. |
| **7** | They strike first. | Ambush, assassinate, purge evidence, complete ritual, flee, or expose the cell publicly. |

### When Enemy Awareness Rises

Raise Enemy Awareness by **1** when:

- A suspect escapes with details.
- The cell repeats a recognizable method.
- An enemy survives an encounter with the cell.
- The cell interrogates, threatens, or surveils too close to the core conspiracy.
- A Shadow fallout specifically points to the enemy learning something.
- The cell uses a distinctive Psychic Power, Forbidden Method, weapon, phrase, or authority claim.

Raise Enemy Awareness by **2** when:

- The enemy captures a cell asset.
- The enemy identifies an acolyte's true name or face.
- The cell reveals Inquisitorial authority to the enemy.
- The cell fails to secure a major prisoner, artifact, or data source.
- A daemon, xenos intelligence, or psyker marks the cell directly.

### Lowering Enemy Awareness

Enemy Awareness is harder to lower than Heat.

It can drop by **1** if the cell:

- Kills, captures, turns, or discredits the specific person who knows.
- Plants a convincing false pattern.
- Burns the exposed method and adopts a new one.
- Uses Patron cleanup, memory work, data erasure, or a forbidden method.
- Lets the enemy draw the wrong conclusion and then reinforces it.

Enemy Awareness should not drop below the highest specific fact the enemy has learned unless that fact is erased, invalidated, or made useless.

## 8. Cover Identities

Every acolyte begins with a Cover Identity. A Cover Identity is a Resource die that explains why the acolyte is present and what they can access.

| Cover Die | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **D3** | Thin, improvised, or temporary. Good for one scene. |
| **D6** | Plausible but limited. Standard starting Cover. |
| **D8** | Well-supported, documented, and useful. |
| **D10** | Deep, witnessed, and institutionally reinforced. |

### Using Cover

Use a Cover Identity when it helps you:

- Enter a location.
- Explain your presence.
- Avoid scrutiny.
- Access records, people, or equipment.
- Redirect suspicion.
- Absorb Shadow stress.
- Gain Mastery on a Deceive, Command, Procure, Investigate, or Skulk roll.

When a Cover is actively tested, roll the relevant action normally. The Cover can add a Resource die, provide Mastery, or absorb stress if the fiction supports it.

### Cover Damage

When a Cover takes damage, choose the most appropriate result.

| Cover Damage | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Scuffed** | Step the Cover down one die size, or take D3 Shadow stress. |
| **Questioned** | The next roll relying on the Cover is Risky. |
| **Flagged** | The Cover works, but its use raises Heat by 1 until repaired. |
| **Compromised** | The Cover only works in limited places or with additional proof. |
| **Exposed** | Burn the Cover or increase Heat by 1. |
| **Collapsed** | Burn the Cover and increase Heat by 1. If tied to the Patron, mark Patron Notice. |

### Burning Cover

A player may burn a Cover Identity to:

- Ignore one Shadow fallout.
- Gain automatic access to a plausible location, person, or file.
- Convert a failed Deceive or Skulk roll into success at cost.
- Reduce Heat by 1 by making the false identity take the blame.
- Protect another acolyte from exposure.

When Cover is burned:

- The Cover is destroyed.
- Heat increases by 1 unless the burn was specifically used to reduce Heat.
- Someone remembers enough to matter later.
- If the Cover was Patron-backed, mark Patron Notice.

### Repairing Cover

To repair a damaged Cover, the cell must spend time, resources, leverage, or favors.

| Repair Method | Effect | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Forge new documents | Restore one die step | Procure or Deceive roll; risk Shadow stress. |
| Bribe records office | Restore one die step | Spend Contact/Resource; risk Authority stress. |
| Add a witness | Restore one die step | Put an NPC at risk. |
| Plant corroborating evidence | Restore one die step | Create future fallout if found. |
| Call Patron cleanup | Restore one or two die steps | Mark Patron Notice or step down a Boon. |
| Burn a lesser alias | Restore one die step | Destroy another Cover or Contact. |

## 9. Cell Cover

Sometimes the cell has a shared cover story: tithe auditors, relic examiners, corpse reclamation officers, void factors, Munitorum inspectors, pilgrim confessors, or sanctioned bounty agents.

A Cell Cover works like a shared Resource die.

| Cell Cover Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **D6** | Plausible entry fiction. |
| **D8** | Supported by Patron, records, and local expectations. |
| **D10** | Deep operation with documents, witnesses, local history, and fallback identities. |

### Cell Cover Uses

The cell may use Cell Cover to:

- Get everyone into a location.
- Explain why multiple acolytes are together.
- Absorb a shared Shadow consequence.
- Lower starting Heat by 1.
- Create individual D6 Covers for the Operation.
- Reduce Heat by 1 when the cover story takes blame.

### Cell Cover Fallout

When Cell Cover collapses, choose two:

- Heat +2.
- All individual Covers tied to it become Risky.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- A local faction becomes hostile.
- A Contact, Bond, or Resource is exposed.
- The next Requisition from the relevant institution is Contested.

## 10. Shadow Fallout and Heat

Shadow fallout often creates Heat, but not always.

| Shadow Fallout | Heat Effect |
|---|---|
| **Witness Chatter** | Heat +1 unless addressed immediately. |
| **Pict Capture** | Heat +1 if the system is monitored or reviewed. |
| **Cover Inconsistency** | No Heat yet; next roll using that Cover is Risky. |
| **Wrong Eyes** | Heat +1 if the faction matters to the Operation. |
| **Alias Compromised** | Heat +1; all rolls relying on it are Dangerous. |
| **Safehouse Watched** | No immediate Heat, but any use of the safehouse raises Heat +1. |
| **Enemy Counter-Infiltration** | Enemy Awareness +1. |
| **Local Law Alerted** | Heat +1 or +2 depending on scale. |
| **Cell Exposed** | Heat jumps to 9 unless the cell burns assets immediately. |
| **Operation Burned** | Heat becomes 9. |
| **Identity Cascade** | Heat +1 and each acolyte names a Bond, Contact, Resource, or Cover at risk. |

### When Shadow Does Not Raise Heat

Do not raise Heat automatically if the exposure is private, contained, or purely personal.

Examples:

- A single acolyte's alias is doubted by one NPC who can still be handled.
- A forged document is flagged but not yet reviewed.
- A Contact knows too much but remains loyal.
- A pict-capture exists in a closed local system the cell can still access.
- An enemy knows something but cannot yet act on it.

In these cases, mark Shadow stress, create a clock, or raise Enemy Awareness instead.

## 11. Witnesses and Evidence

When a Situation ends messily, identify what remains.

### Witness Categories

| Witness | Risk |
|---|---|
| **Panicked Civilian** | Creates rumor, inaccurate testimony, public fear. |
| **Trained Official** | Creates paperwork, jurisdiction, factional complaint. |
| **Enemy Survivor** | Raises Enemy Awareness. |
| **Servitor / Machine Witness** | Leaves data logs, pict-capture, auspex trace. |
| **Psyker / Astropath** | Leaves psychic impression, soul-mark, dream echo. |
| **Noble / Priest / Tech-Priest** | Creates political consequence beyond the scene. |
| **Bond / Contact** | Creates emotional and practical leverage over the cell. |

### Evidence Categories

| Evidence | Risk |
|---|---|
| **Bodies** | Cause investigations, martyrdom, plague, autopsy, or questions. |
| **Weapon Traces** | Reveal method, faction, restricted gear, or Inquisition pattern. |
| **Pict / Vox Records** | Can spread quickly, be edited, or be planted. |
| **Cogitator Logs** | Machine-spirit memory, access timestamps, noospheric traces. |
| **Psychic Residue** | Detectable by psykers, witches, daemons, exorcists, and wards. |
| **Forbidden Artifacts** | Incriminating by existence alone. |
| **Confessions** | Useful, unreliable, politically dangerous. |
| **Paper Trails** | Slow, hard to erase, and deadly when finally assembled. |

### Evidence Procedure

At the end of a messy Situation, ask:

1. What physical evidence remains?
2. What institutional evidence remains?
3. Who saw the cell?
4. Who can connect the event to the Inquisition?
5. Who benefits if this evidence spreads?
6. Who can erase, alter, steal, or weaponize it?

Then decide whether to:

- Increase Heat.
- Increase Enemy Awareness.
- Mark Shadow stress.
- Tick a clock.
- Create a new clue.
- Create a new threat.

## 12. Reducing Heat

Heat can be reduced, but never for free.

| Method | Heat Reduction | Cost |
|---|---:|---|
| Burn a Cover Identity | -1 | The Cover is destroyed and someone remembers enough to matter. |
| Silence witnesses | -1 | Take Mind, Shadow, or Authority stress; create a Humanity or Radical Beat opportunity. |
| Plant false evidence | -1 | Someone innocent or useful is endangered. |
| Frame another faction | -1 or -2 | Create an enemy or future complication. |
| Call in Patron cleanup | -1 or -2 | Mark Patron Notice or step down a Boon. |
| Lay low | -1 | The enemy advances a clock or completes a move. |
| Sacrifice a Contact, Bond, or Resource | -1 or -2 | Reduce its die step or break it. |
| Publicly redirect suspicion | -1 | Take Authority or Shadow stress. |
| Destroy records | -1 | Lose a clue, asset, or proof. |
| Make a scapegoat | -1 | Create future guilt, revenge, or investigation. |
| Trigger a controlled purge | -2 | Heat becomes official violence; lose subtle options. |
| Reveal a lesser truth | -1 | A greater truth remains hidden, but someone suffers for the partial one. |

Heat cannot usually be reduced below the highest threshold already crossed during the current Operation unless the cell makes a major sacrifice or changes the whole operational frame.

Example: if the cell has reached Heat 6, ordinary cleanup can reduce Heat to 5 or 6 at best. To return to Ghosts, they need to end the Operation, burn major assets, or fundamentally change identities and location.

## 13. Heat and Difficulty

Heat can change the Difficulty of actions.

| Heat State | Default Pressure |
|---|---|
| **Ghosts** | Covert actions are usually Safe if the Cover makes sense. Loud actions may still be Risky. |
| **Rumors** | Rolls relying on Cover, quiet access, or social plausibility become Risky when scrutiny is relevant. |
| **Hunted** | Covert actions against alerted targets are Risky or Dangerous. Requisitions may be Contested. |
| **Burned** | Quiet actions are usually Dangerous or impossible until the cell changes the situation. Loud actions may be safer than subtle ones. |

Heat does not make every roll harder. It makes the rolls affected by exposure harder.

A firefight in a Burned Operation might be Safe if the Secutor has position and surprise. Sneaking out afterward is probably Dangerous.

---

## 14. Heat and Requisition

The higher the Heat, the harder it is to acquire help without consequence.

| Heat State | Requisition Procedure |
|---|---|
| **Ghosts** | Routine if the request fits Cover or Patron Boons. |
| **Rumors** | Costly if the request creates records or witnesses. |
| **Hunted** | Contested unless backed by strong authority or a prepared Contact. |
| **Burned** | The request works only if loud, official, desperate, forbidden, or sacrificial. |

At Heat 6+, any Requisition from local institutions risks:

- Authority stress.
- Shadow stress.
- Patron Notice.
- A factional complaint.
- A demand for proof.
- A Liability trigger.

At Heat 9+, Requisitions should either be emergency assets or consequences in disguise.

## 15. Heat and Patron Pressure

Heat is not just enemy exposure. It is also evidence of how well or badly the cell is serving the Patron's preferred method.

Mark Patron Notice when:

- Heat reaches 3, 6, or 9 for the first time.
- Heat rises because of Rosette use.
- Heat rises because of a Patron Boon.
- Heat rises because of a Red Line violation.
- Heat reaches 6+ during an Operation with **Preserve Subtlety Above All**.
- Heat reaches 9 for any reason.

Lose 1 Patience when:

- Heat reaches 9.
- Heat exposes the Patron's name.
- Heat burns a Patron Cover Story.
- Heat causes another Imperial faction to complain, intervene, or investigate.
- Heat makes the Mandate harder to complete.

### Patron Reactions by Heat

| Heat State | Likely Patron Reaction |
|---|---|
| **Ghosts** | Trusts the cell to continue. May provide quiet Boons. |
| **Rumors** | Requests status, proof, or caution. May impose a Red Line. |
| **Hunted** | Demands progress, cleaner methods, or decisive action. May send an observer. |
| **Burned** | Threatens disavowal, recalls the cell, sends a rival, or authorizes escalation. |

## 16. Heat and Operation Clocks

Heat should make the mission move.

Each Operation should have at least one opposition clock. When Heat rises, the GM may tick an appropriate clock instead of, or in addition to, a Heat Move.

| Clock | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Cult Completes the Rite** | The enemy reaches their occult objective. |
| **Noble House Sanitizes Evidence** | Records, witnesses, and bodies disappear. |
| **Arbites Lockdown** | Movement becomes impossible without authority. |
| **Rival Cell Intervenes** | Another Inquisitorial agenda enters the field. |
| **Public Panic** | The situation becomes a riot, stampede, or witch-hunt. |
| **Daemon Manifests** | The threat becomes impossible to hide. |
| **Xenos Signal Sent** | Something beyond the system receives the call. |
| **Mechanicus Claims Jurisdiction** | Tech evidence, bodies, machines, and witnesses vanish into forge custody. |
| **Ecclesiarchy Controls the Narrative** | The event becomes miracle, heresy, or martyrdom. |
| **Patron Audit Arrives** | A superior asset enters play and constrains the cell. |

### Clock Ticks from Heat

| Heat Event | Clock Effect |
|---|---|
| Heat rises by 1 | Tick one relevant clock if the enemy can react. |
| Heat reaches 3 | Tick a clock or introduce a new one. |
| Heat reaches 6 | Tick a clock twice or make a direct enemy move. |
| Heat reaches 9 | Fill a clock, split the Operation, or force a crisis choice. |

## 17. Heat and Beats

Heat should create Beat opportunities.

### Duty Beat Opportunities

- Invoke Imperial authority to contain public panic.
- Destroy evidence too dangerous to preserve.
- Sacrifice a Cover, Contact, or Bond for the Mandate.
- Call in force despite collateral consequences.
- Obey the Patron's cleanup order.
- Give the Patron a prisoner, corpse, confession, or scapegoat.

### Humanity Beat Opportunities

- Spare a witness instead of silencing them.
- Tell civilians enough truth to let them survive.
- Protect a Contact whose existence now endangers the Operation.
- Prevent an Imperial faction from overreacting.
- Take blame to spare someone innocent.
- Let a compromised but sympathetic person escape.

### Radical Beat Opportunities

- Plant false evidence to lower Heat.
- Use psychic or forbidden methods to erase witnesses or memories.
- Preserve a forbidden asset instead of logging it.
- Ally with a criminal, heretic, mutant, or xenos source to escape exposure.
- Let the enemy advance to preserve cover.
- Hide the true cause of the exposure from Patron and cell alike.

## 18. Flashing the Rosette

Open Inquisitorial authority is a Heat tool.

When an acolyte flashes the Rosette or equivalent authority, choose one immediate benefit:

- Gain Mastery on Command, Procure, or Investigate.
- Turn a failed Requisition into success at cost.
- Force an Imperial official to comply.
- Bypass ordinary legal restrictions.
- Command immediate access to a location, prisoner, archive, or restricted object.
- Seize jurisdiction from a local faction.
- Freeze a scene long enough for the cell to act.

Then choose one cost:

- Heat +1 if private or contained.
- Heat +2 if public, recorded, or politically explosive.
- D6 Authority stress.
- D6 Shadow stress.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- Trigger a Patron Liability.
- Create a factional enemy.

If the Rosette is used at Heat 6+, the GM should also ask:

**Who challenges, reports, exploits, or counterfeits this authority?**

## 19. Going Loud

Sometimes subtlety fails or becomes less important than speed.

When the cell chooses to go loud, declare what quiet objective is being abandoned or put at risk.

Then choose one loud advantage:

- All acolytes gain Mastery on the first violent or commanding action.
- The cell bypasses one obstacle through force or authority.
- A Patron Boon can be used at full die without a Requisition roll.
- The enemy loses the chance to hide one clue, prisoner, or artifact.
- Civilians, officials, or lesser enemies scatter.
- A target is forced into the open.

Then apply two costs:

- Heat becomes 9.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- Tick an enemy clock twice.
- Destroy or endanger a clue.
- Burn a Cover Identity.
- Create civilian casualties or witnesses.
- Trigger a faction response.
- Make the next Mission start at +1 Heat in the same theater.

Going loud should feel powerful.

It should also change the campaign.

---

## 20. Retreating from an Operation

The cell may retreat from an Operation to preserve lives, cover, or resources.

When they retreat, choose one benefit:

- Clear D3 Shadow stress from each acolyte.
- Reduce Heat by 1.
- Preserve one Cover, Bond, Contact, or Boon that would otherwise be lost.
- Carry one clue out safely.
- Prevent one Heat 9 move from fully landing.

Then choose one cost:

- The enemy advances a clock.
- The Core Clue moves, mutates, or becomes harder to reach.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- Lose 1 Patience if the Patron demanded immediate success.
- A Bond, Contact, or local community suffers in the cell's absence.
- The next Operation starts at +1 Heat.

Retreat is not failure. It is a choice about what the cell values more than immediate success.

---

## 21. Ending an Operation

At the end of an Operation, resolve Heat.

### If Heat is 0-2: Ghost Exit

Choose one:

- Clear Heat completely.
- Preserve one Cover or Contact from future suspicion.
- Gain Mastery on the first roll of the next Operation because the cell controls the tempo.
- Carry Heat forward as a hidden clock instead of public exposure.

### If Heat is 3-5: Rumor Exit

Choose one:

- Start the next related Operation at Heat 1.
- Create a rumor, witness, or faction curiosity.
- Mark D3 Shadow stress to one acolyte most tied to the exposure.
- The enemy starts at Awareness 1 if they were plausibly affected.

### If Heat is 6-8: Hunted Exit

Choose two:

- Start the next related Operation at Heat 2.
- Increase Enemy Awareness by 1.
- Mark Patron Notice.
- Put one Cover, Bond, Contact, Resource, or Boon at risk.
- Tick a campaign clock.

### If Heat is 9+: Burned Exit

Choose three:

- Lose 1 Patron Patience.
- Start the next related Operation at Heat 3.
- Increase Enemy Awareness by 2.
- Burn one Cover or Contact.
- Trigger a factional complaint or rival intervention.
- Tick a campaign clock twice.
- The public story becomes a lie that someone powerful controls.
- The cell must accept a Patron correction, audit, or Forbidden Offer.

---

## 22. Between-Operations Cleanup

Between Operations, the cell may attempt cleanup.

Each acolyte may choose one cleanup action, or the cell may take one group cleanup action.

| Cleanup Action | Effect | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Erase Records** | Reduce Heat by 1 or remove an evidence trail. | Operate/Deceive; Shadow or Authority stress. |
| **Silence Witnesses** | Reduce Heat by 1 or prevent Enemy Awareness increase. | Mind, Authority, or Corruption stress depending method. |
| **Repair Cover** | Restore one Cover die step. | Resource cost or Shadow stress. |
| **Redirect Suspicion** | Reduce Heat by 1 and create a scapegoat. | Humanity cost, future enemy. |
| **Confess to Patron** | Clear Patron Notice or restore Patience if useful. | Correction order, audit, or Authority stress. |
| **Use Contacts** | Learn who is asking questions. | Contact steps down if exposed. |
| **Lay Low** | Clear Heat by 1 and stress by D3. | Enemy advances a clock. |
| **Purge Evidence** | Destroy a dangerous object, body, or file. | Lose a clue or Radical opportunity. |

If the cleanup action targets a problem established in play, it should work on success. On 7 or lower, it works with a cost.

## 23. GM End-of-Situation Procedure

At the end of each Situation, ask these questions aloud or silently:

1. Did anyone see what happened?
2. Did anyone record what happened?
3. Did anyone survive with useful information?
4. Did the cell use loud violence, psychic power, forbidden tools, or Inquisitorial authority?
5. Did a Cover Identity explain the event?
6. Did the enemy learn something specific?
7. Did the Patron have reason to notice?
8. Did any faction gain a reason to intervene?
9. Did any evidence remain unsecured?
10. Did the cell cross or approach a Red Line?

Then:

1. Adjust Heat.
2. Adjust Enemy Awareness if needed.
3. Apply Shadow stress or Fallout if needed.
4. Trigger threshold moves if Heat reached 3, 6, or 9.
5. Mark Patron Notice if appropriate.
6. Tick any Operation clocks.
7. Announce what the players can perceive changing.

Always tell the players the visible signs of Heat rising. Do not hide every consequence. They should feel the net tightening.

---

## 24. Player-Facing Heat Summary

Players should know this version:

| Heat | Player-Facing Meaning |
|---:|---|
| **0-2: Ghosts** | We are hidden. We choose the moment. |
| **3-5: Rumors** | Someone noticed. Cover still works, but scrutiny begins. |
| **6-8: Hunted** | Someone is acting against us. Safehouses, Contacts, and Covers are at risk. |
| **9+: Burned** | The Operation cannot stay quiet. Finish loud, retreat, sacrifice, or change the objective. |

Heat rises when the cell leaves:

- Witnesses
- Records
- Bodies
- Psychic traces
- Public authority
- Forbidden evidence
- Obvious violence
- Compromised cover
- Factional complaints

Heat can be reduced, but only by spending something:

- Cover
- Time
- Contacts
- Patron favor
- Evidence
- Innocence
- Truth

## 25. Example: Administratum Audit Cover

The cell enters Hive Carcosa as Administratum tithe auditors.

**Starting Heat:** 1

The Cover Story is plausible, but the hive is tense because tithe shipments are missing.

### Situation 1: Ledger Access

The Explicator uses the audit cover to access shipping ledgers.

The roll succeeds at cost.

The GM marks D3 Shadow stress because a clerk notices the seal format is outdated.

Heat does not rise yet. The problem is private and contained.

### Situation 2: Underhive Ambush

The cell follows a shipment route and is ambushed by cult enforcers.

The Secutor uses a boltgun in a crowded hab-market.

Heat rises by 2:

- Loud restricted weapon
- Public violence
- Witnesses

Heat goes from 1 to 3.

The GM makes a Rumors move: a local enforcer asks why tithe auditors are carrying military-grade weapons.

### Situation 3: Rosette Threat

The Interrogator privately invokes Inquisitorial authority to force the enforcer to hand over pict records.

The benefit works.

Heat rises by 1, and Patron Notice is marked because Patron Vhal demanded discretion.

Heat is now 4.

### Situation 4: Cleanup

The Penumbra burns a disposable Munitorum sub-cover to explain the firefight as a black-market arms seizure.

The Cover is destroyed.

Heat drops by 1 to 3, but someone in the Munitorum now has a reason to check the story later.

The Operation continues, but the cell is no longer invisible.

---

## 26. Example: Burned Occult Containment

The cell investigates a shrine miracle.

A Sanctioned Psyker uses Witch-Sight and sees a warp anchor behind the reliquary wall. The Exorcist begins a containment rite.

The roll is Dangerous.

It succeeds at cost.

The reliquary stops bleeding light, but every candle in the shrine burns black and every pilgrim hears their dead mother's voice.

Heat rises by 2:

- Public psychic/warp phenomenon
- Many witnesses

Heat reaches 6.

The GM makes a Hunted move: the Ecclesiarchy takes control of the shrine and assigns confessors to interview all witnesses.

Enemy Awareness does not rise yet because the daemon does not know the cell's names, but a new clock begins:

**Ecclesiarchy Controls the Narrative: 1 / 4**

The cell can continue, but any further action in the shrine is now under religious scrutiny.

Command + Ecclesiarchy might help.

Deceive without a good Cover is Risky or Dangerous.

---

## 27. Design Goal

Heat should not punish action. It should change the shape of action.

At low Heat, the cell chooses where and when to strike.

At medium Heat, the opposition starts asking questions.

At high Heat, the cell is no longer the only active hunter.

At Burned, the Operation becomes a crisis:

**purge, flee, expose, negotiate, sacrifice, or finish the job loud.**

The core question is never:

**Can the acolytes avoid consequences?**

The question is:

**Which consequences do they choose, and who pays for them?**
