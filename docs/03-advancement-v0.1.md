# Advancement v0.1

Acolytes do not advance because they kill enemies. They advance because they make meaningful choices under pressure.

Acolytes grow when they obey terrible orders, preserve or betray their humanity, use forbidden methods, protect the cell, damage their cover, burn resources, learn truths they cannot unlearn, or change the mission, the world, or themselves.

Every advance should answer one of these questions:

- What did you prove you are willing to do?
- What did the mission teach you?
- What part of you did the Inquisition sharpen?
- What part of you did it break?

---

## 1. Beat Categories

At the start of each session, each player chooses **three Beats** from the lists below (or from their class-specific beats, see class entries).

**At least one Beat must come from a different category than the other two.** Beyond that, you choose the mix. A Puritan might take 2 Duty and 1 Radical. A heretic-in-waiting might take 2 Radical and 1 Humanity. The combination tells the table who the acolyte is becoming.

You may substitute a **class-specific Beat** for any slot. Class beats are printed in your class entry.

| Beat Type | Theme |
|---|---|
| **Duty** | Obedience, purity, mission, command, sacrifice, official violence |
| **Humanity** | Mercy, loyalty, doubt, civilian life, confession, restraint |
| **Radical** | Forbidden tools, expedience, secrecy, corruption, leverage, survival |

Players may choose any mix of Minor and Major Beats available to them.

Severe Beats are special. They should usually be chosen only after the acolyte has completed at least **four total Beats**, taken **Severe Fallout**, or reached a major mission turning point.

---

## 2. Completing Beats

A Beat is completed when the player deliberately brings it into the fiction and the table agrees that it mattered.

A Beat does **not** require success.

If your Beat is “Spare someone dangerous,” and that person escapes, you still completed the Beat. The point is that your acolyte chose mercy when mercy had a cost.

If your Beat is “Invoke Inquisitorial authority publicly,” and it ruins the cell’s cover, you still completed the Beat. The point is that your acolyte chose power over subtlety.

Once completed, cross that Beat off. You cannot complete the same Beat again with the same character unless a class ability, Patron move, or campaign rule refreshes it.

---

## 3. Advancement Rewards

When you complete a Beat, immediately gain the listed reward.

| Beat Completed | Reward |
|---|---|
| **Minor Beat** | Minor Advance or D6 Requisition |
| **Major Beat** | Major Advance or D8 Requisition |
| **Severe Beat** | Severe Advance, D10 Requisition, or permanent Patron Boon |

A Requisition may be equipment, a contact, a temporary asset, safehouse access, a specialist, forbidden intelligence, or faction support.

Acolytes may bank **one unspent Advance**. If they would gain another while already holding one, they must spend one immediately or convert the new reward into a Requisition.

---

## 4. Spending Advances

### Minor Advances

Minor Advances are useful, narrow, and character-defining without changing the whole campaign.

| Minor Advance | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Class Minor Ability** | Gain one Minor Ability from your Class. |
| **New Skill** | Gain one new Skill, if fictionally justified by training or experience. |
| **New Domain** | Gain one new Domain, if fictionally justified. |
| **Improve Protection** | Gain +1 Protection to one Resistance, tied to gear, training, cover, or conditioning. |
| **Strengthen Bond** | Increase one Bond by one die step, maximum D10. |
| **Strengthen Cover** | Increase one Cover Identity by one die step, maximum D10. |
| **Acquire Contact** | Gain a new D6 Contact. |
| **Acquire Resource** | Gain a new D6 Resource. |
| **Learn Forbidden Method** | Gain one Forbidden Method, if the fiction supports it. |
| **Learn Psychic Power** | Gain one Psychic Power, if you are a psyker or have a specific source of power. |

### Major Advances

Major Advances should change how the acolyte approaches missions.

| Major Advance | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Class Major Ability** | Gain one Major Ability from your Class. |
| **Specialist Mastery** | Choose one Skill + Domain pairing. Gain Mastery when acting under a narrow, named circumstance. |
| **Patron Boon** | Gain a persistent D8 asset from your Patron, such as clearance, a kill-team, a savant, a void-route, or restricted archives. |
| **Forbidden Asset** | Gain a D8 forbidden Resource, such as a xenos device, bound informant, daemonological index, unsanctioned psyker, or heretek tool. |
| **Cell Infrastructure** | Create or upgrade a safehouse, dead-drop network, false-front office, medicae suite, shrine cover, or surveillance post to D8. |
| **Deep Cover** | Create a D8 Cover Identity with supporting documents, witnesses, and local history. |
| **Resistance Hardening** | Gain +2 Protection to one Resistance, or +1 Protection to two Resistances. |
| **Cross-Class Minor Ability** | Gain a Minor Ability from another Class, if you have a teacher, experience, or narrative justification. |
| **Advanced Psychic Power** | Gain a stronger Psychic Power or improve an existing one. |
| **Special Requisition Right** | Once per Mission, gain a D8 Requisition from a specific faction without rolling, but using it risks Authority or Shadow stress. |

### Severe Advances

Severe Advances are transformations. They should be rare, campaign-altering, and possibly character-ending later.

| Severe Advance | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Class Severe Ability** | Gain one Severe or Capstone Ability from your Class. |
| **Rosette-Bearer** | You are elevated toward Interrogator status. Gain Authority Protection 2 and one Patron Boon, but you are now directly accountable. |
| **Sanctioned Monster** | Your forbidden nature becomes officially tolerated. Gain a D10 forbidden Resource or power, but your Patron owns proof of what you are. |
| **Burned and Reborn** | Destroy one Cover, Bond, or identity. Create a new D10 Cover or Shadow asset. |
| **Martyr Reputation** | Gain D10 Authority or Inquisition Resource among those who believe in your sacrifice. Gain a rival or cult that interprets it differently. |
| **Radical Doctrine** | Choose one Forbidden Method. When using it, gain Mastery and reduce its stress cost by one die step. If exposed, treat this as Severe Authority fallout. |
| **Puritan Mandate** | Against one category of enemy — daemon, rogue psyker, heretek, mutant cult, xenos infiltrator — gain Mastery when refusing compromise or mercy. |
| **Living Evidence** | You become the key witness, proof, or survivor of a major heresy. Gain D10 Inquisition leverage, but enemies and allies both need you controlled. |
| **Retirement Clause** | Your acolyte may leave field play alive, becoming a Patron Asset, Contact, or future complication. Create a new acolyte with one extra Minor Advance. |

## 5. Cross-Class Advancement

You may take abilities from another Class, but it should cost more and require a fictional bridge.

| Desired Ability | Required Advance |
|---|---|
| Minor Ability from your Class | Minor Advance |
| Major Ability from your Class | Major Advance |
| Minor Ability from another Class | Major Advance |
| Major Ability from another Class | Severe Advance |
| Severe Ability from another Class | Not normally available |

You must explain how you learned it.

Examples:

- A Secutor trains under a Penumbra to learn a stealth ability.
- An Explicator studies with a Chirurgeon to read biological evidence.
- A Hierophant learns anti-daemon rites from an Exorcist.
- A Radical Asset learns command procedure from a Legate.

When you take a cross-class ability, also add one complication tied to the source.

---

## 6. Requisitions as Advancement

Sometimes an acolyte does not learn a new ability. Sometimes they gain access.

A Requisition may be chosen instead of an Advance.

| Requisition Tier | Examples |
|---|---|
| **D6 Minor Requisition** | Clean papers, medicae supplies, informant, servo-skull, safehouse for one night, local map, squad vox access |
| **D8 Major Requisition** | Arbites cordon, storm trooper fireteam, restricted archive access, psyber-familiar, forensic servitor, voidship extraction, noble invitation |
| **D10 Severe Requisition** | Orbital strike authority, kill-team, full data-vault access, Black Ship intervention, daemon containment cell, planetary lockdown, assassin support |

Requisitions can be temporary or persistent.

A temporary Requisition is powerful and disappears after use.

A persistent Requisition remains available, but can take stress, suffer fallout, be targeted, or demand repayment.

## 7. Patron Notice

The Inquisition notices advancement.

Each time an acolyte completes a **Major** or **Severe** Beat, mark **Patron Notice**.

When the cell reaches **3 Patron Notice**, the Patron makes a move.

| D6 | Patron Move |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Commendation:** The cell gains a D8 Requisition for the next Operation. |
| 2 | **Correction:** The Patron issues a new restriction, doctrine, or Red Line. |
| 3 | **Audit:** A savant, Interrogator, servo-skull, or rival acolyte observes the cell. |
| 4 | **Escalation:** The Patron reveals the threat is worse than stated. Heat increases by 1. |
| 5 | **Demand:** The Patron requires a prisoner, artifact, confession, corpse, or clean record. |
| 6 | **Suspicion:** Each acolyte who completed a Radical Beat since the last Patron Move marks D3 Authority or Shadow stress. |

After the Patron Move, clear Patron Notice.

This is not only a punishment. Sometimes being noticed brings resources. Sometimes it brings chains.

## 8. Duty Beats

Duty Beats reward obedience, orthodoxy, command, sacrifice, official violence, and mission-first thinking.

### Minor Duty Beats

- Invoke Imperial law over local custom.
- Threaten a civilian, subordinate, or official into obedience.
- Destroy evidence because it is too dangerous to preserve.
- Refuse comfort, rest, or safety because the mission continues.
- Report a fellow acolyte's questionable behavior.
- Accept punishment or penance without protest.
- Use official authority even though subtlety would be safer.
- Execute a confirmed heretic, mutant, traitor, or xenos asset.
- Prioritize the mission over a Bond.
- Demand supplies or cooperation from people who can barely afford it.
- Publicly name something as heresy.
- Obey a direct order that makes the situation worse for someone innocent.
- Take Body, Mind, or Authority stress while upholding doctrine.
- Let a lesser evil continue because the Patron's objective matters more.
- Refuse a bargain with a forbidden source.
- Destroy a piece of equipment that has become compromising or doctrinally suspect.
- Requisition official gear through proper channels when a faster, dirtier option exists.

### Major Duty Beats

- Lead a purge, raid, or seizure under Inquisitorial authority.
- Sacrifice a Bond, Cover, or Contact to preserve the mission.
- Condemn an Imperial official in public.
- Destroy a powerful artifact instead of studying or exploiting it.
- Deny mercy to someone dangerous who might have been useful.
- Enforce orthodoxy even when it meaningfully harms the Operation.
- Deliver a prisoner, witness, or asset to your Patron knowing they will suffer.
- Choose the Patron's order over the cell's judgment.
- Cause a Bond to take Major or Severe fallout for reasons of duty.
- Accept Severe Authority fallout rather than compromise the chain of command.

### Severe Duty Beats

- Call down overwhelming Imperial force and accept the collateral damage.
- Expose your cell to save the mission's official objective.
- Execute or condemn a fellow acolyte for corruption, treason, or disobedience.
- Allow a community, haven, or civilian population to be destroyed to contain the threat.
- Become the Patron's instrument so completely that your old life is unrecoverable.

## 9. Humanity Beats

Humanity Beats reward mercy, doubt, restraint, loyalty, civilian lives, and personal connection.

### Minor Humanity Beats

- Protect a vulnerable NPC by delaying the mission.
- Tell the truth when a lie would protect your authority.
- Comfort someone doctrine says should be ignored or condemned.
- Refuse to harm someone begging for mercy.
- Preserve evidence because people deserve the truth.
- Admit fear, guilt, doubt, or regret to another acolyte.
- De-escalate a situation the cell was ready to solve with violence.
- Help someone escape unjust punishment.
- Treat a heretic, mutant, criminal, or enemy as a person with a history.
- Give away a needed Resource for another's survival.
- Protect a Bond at mission cost.
- Follow local custom instead of Imperial procedure.
- Spare a useful but compromised witness.
- Intervene to stop cruelty by an Imperial authority.
- Take Mind, Shadow, or Authority stress to preserve someone's dignity.

### Major Humanity Beats

- Risk the Operation to save innocents.
- Publicly defy an inhumane order.
- Spare a dangerous enemy who could return later.
- Reveal deep vulnerability to the entire cell.
- Build trust with a community devastated by Imperial policy or hidden corruption.
- Advocate for mercy before someone who outranks you.
- Abandon a tactical advantage to protect a single life.
- Take responsibility for harm the cell caused.
- Break cover to save a Bond.
- Accept Severe Mind or Shadow fallout rather than betray someone who trusted you.

### Severe Humanity Beats

- Betray or defy your Patron to prevent an atrocity.
- Save a community that Imperial doctrine says should be purged.
- Reveal the truth of the mission to people who are not cleared for it.
- Let a dangerous enemy live because killing them would make you something worse.
- Choose the cell, a Bond, or innocent lives over your future in the Inquisition.

---

## 10. Radical Beats

Radical Beats reward forbidden knowledge, expedience, secrecy, compromise, corruption, and the terrible logic of “necessary” evil.

### Minor Radical Beats

- Use a Forbidden Method.
- Keep a forbidden artifact instead of destroying it.
- Hide evidence from your Patron or cell.
- Lie to another acolyte to preserve your own agenda.
- Accept help from a heretic, mutant, xenos, rogue psyker, or criminal.
- Use excessive force when restraint was possible.
- Read, preserve, or copy a proscribed text.
- Exploit a vulnerable community, witness, or Bond.
- Destroy evidence to protect yourself or the cell.
- Allow a dangerous secret to remain unreported.
- Use a psychic power in a way that frightens witnesses.
- Let someone else take blame for your failure.
- Trade with the underworld for mission advantage.
- Conceal your own corruption, fear, or forbidden knowledge.
- Take Corruption, Shadow, or Authority stress while using an unclean solution.
- Rely on a negative aspect of forbidden equipment to achieve a desperate goal.
- Modify, upgrade, or corrupt a piece of equipment beyond its sanctioned design.

### Major Radical Beats

- Form a tacit or explicit alliance with a heretic, xenos, mutant, rogue psyker, apostate, or criminal power.
- Use forbidden knowledge to save the mission.
- Use forbidden knowledge to save an acolyte.
- Betray a trusted ally for strategic advantage.
- Reject Imperial doctrine in a moment of crisis.
- Destroy proof of corruption because exposure would cause worse damage.
- Sacrifice a Bond to advance a personal or radical agenda.
- Enable a condemned enemy to escape justice.
- Use a xenos, daemonic, or heretek device in the field.
- Accept Severe Corruption or Authority fallout to preserve a radical asset.

### Severe Radical Beats

- Bind, unleash, consult, or preserve a daemonhost or equivalent abomination.
- Choose a forbidden solution when an orthodox solution exists but would cost more lives.
- Secretly turn the mission toward your own agenda.
- Protect a source of corruption because it is too useful to destroy.
- Become something your Patron would execute if they fully understood you.

## 11. End-of-Mission Change Awards

Beats are the main advancement engine.

At the end of a Mission, the GM may also award one extra Advance to each acolyte based on what the cell changed. Use this sparingly.

| Change Caused | Award |
|---|---|
| **Small but real change** | Minor Advance |
| **Major operational change** | Major Advance |
| **Sector-shaking or irreversible change** | Severe Advance |

Examples:

| Mission Outcome | Award |
|---|---|
| Exposed a cult cell but left its masters hidden | Minor |
| Destroyed a noble-backed cult network | Major |
| Caused a planetary purge, succession crisis, or Inquisitorial schism | Severe |
| Saved a settlement the Patron expected to write off | Major |
| Preserved a forbidden asset that changes future missions | Major or Severe |
| Burned the cell's existence across the sector | Severe |

---

## 12. Advancement Limits

### Starting Limit

A starting acolyte may not have more than:

- 4 Skills
- 4 Domains
- 2 Forbidden Domains
- 2 Psychic Powers
- 2 Forbidden Methods

### Long-Term Limit

Once an acolyte has **6 total Advances**, they must choose one path:

| Path | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Promotion** | Move toward Interrogator status. Gain Authority pressure. |
| **Radicalization** | Gain forbidden power. Gain Patron suspicion. |
| **Burnout** | Gain survival tools. Refresh becomes harder. |
| **Martyrdom** | Gain Duty power. Humanity becomes harder to preserve. |
| **Exit Strategy** | Build toward retirement, disappearance, or betrayal. |

After **10 total Advances**, the table should ask whether the acolyte is still playable in the same mode.

They may continue, but the game should acknowledge that they are no longer merely an acolyte. They are becoming an Interrogator, monster, martyr, rogue asset, or future antagonist.

## 13. Example Advancement

Mara (an Explicator) chooses:

- **Humanity Beat:** Treat a heretic as a person with a history.
- **Radical Beat:** Hide evidence from your Patron or cell.
- **Class Beat:** Connect two separate clues into a case file. *(Explicator class-specific beat, replacing her third slot.)*

Note that Mara picked 1 Humanity, 1 Radical, and 1 class beat — this satisfies the rule that at least one Beat must come from a different category than the others.

During the session, Mara interrogates a captured cult courier and realizes the courier joined the cult after the Administratum falsified famine records. Mara treats him humanely and learns the cult's route through the corpse-starch office.

She completes her **Humanity Beat** and gains a **Minor Advance**.

Later, she pockets the courier's old cult token instead of logging it as evidence. She completes her **Radical Beat** and takes a **D6 Requisition** instead of a Minor Advance: the token can be used once as a D6 Resource to pass as cult-adjacent.

She does not complete her class Beat this session.

At the start of next session, she may choose new Beats. The uncompleted class Beat returns to the pool.
