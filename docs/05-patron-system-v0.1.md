# Patron System v0.1

The Patron is the cell's shared campaign engine: authority, resources, ideology, pressure, and judgment.

A good Patron should make the players think:

- **We need them.**
- **We fear them.**
- **We are not sure they are right.**

---

## 1. What the Patron Does

Your Patron is not just the mission-giver.

| Function | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Authority** | The cell acts in their name, openly or covertly. |
| **Resource Pipeline** | They provide Boons, Requisitions, contacts, archives, orders, and cover. |
| **Ideological Pressure** | Their Ordo and Philosophy shape what counts as success. |
| **Threat** | They judge failure, disobedience, corruption, exposure, and inconvenient mercy. |
| **Mystery** | They are hiding something from the cell. |

---

## 2. Patron Creation Summary

To create a Patron:

1. Choose an **Ordo**.
2. Choose a public **Philosophy**.
3. Choose or define a private **Method**.
4. Choose a **Mandate**.
5. Choose **three Boons**.
6. Choose **three Liabilities**.
7. Set **Patience**.
8. Define **Red Lines**.
9. Define the Patron's **Secret**.
10. Create one **Standing Order**.
11. Create one **Forbidden Offer**.

## 4. Ordo

The Patron's Ordo defines the broad category of threat they hunt.

### Ordo Hereticus

Hunters of witches, rebels, mutants, cults, seditionists, apostates, corrupt officials, rogue psykers, and internal rot.

**Preferred Domains:** Heresy, Imperium, Ecclesiarchy, Inquisition  
**Common Boons:** Arbites ties, Ministorum contacts, confessor network, witch-hunter assets, Black Ship access, spy network  
**Common Liabilities:** Demands Discretion, Paranoid, Hallowed Ignorance, Enemy, Hidden Philosophy, Loose Cannon  
**Default Red Line:** Never allow a heretical movement to become public sympathy.  
**Ordo Move — Root Out the Rot:** Once per Mission, the Patron may provide a D8 Resource to identify, expose, isolate, or purge a human conspiracy. Using it usually risks Authority or Shadow.

### Ordo Xenos

Hunters of aliens, xenotech, genestealer cults, alien infiltrators, forbidden trade, and human collaboration with xenos.

**Preferred Domains:** Xenos, Void, Inquisition, Mechanicus  
**Common Boons:** Deathwatch sanction, xenotech vault, void contacts, forbidden archive, hunting hounds, plausible deniability  
**Common Liabilities:** Offended the Omnissiah, Xenogenetics, Enemy Operatives, Suspect, Sanction Denied, Unorthodox Beliefs  
**Default Red Line:** Never let alien influence become normalized, useful, or sympathetic.  
**Ordo Move — Containment Doctrine:** Once per Mission, the Patron may provide a D8 Resource to contain, identify, translate, or destroy a xenos threat. If the cell exploits rather than destroys the threat, mark Patron Notice.

### Ordo Malleus

Hunters of daemons, possession, warp breaches, sorcery, daemon cults, cursed relics, and realities that must not be named.

**Preferred Domains:** Warp, Heresy, Inquisition, Ecclesiarchy  
**Common Boons:** daemonological archive, exorcist cadre, warded sanctum, containment rites, sanctioned psyker support, forbidden grimoire  
**Common Liabilities:** Hidden Philosophy, Paranoid, Demands Discretion, Tainted Asset, Nemesis, Forbidden Knowledge  
**Default Red Line:** Never reveal the full truth of the daemonic to the uninitiated.  
**Ordo Move — Seal the Breach:** Once per Mission, the Patron may provide a D8 Resource to ward, bind, banish, contain, or erase evidence of a warp threat. Using it publicly increases Heat by 1.

### Obscure Ordo

A minor Ordo, splinter conclave, personal crusade, or unofficial network focused on a narrower threat.

| Ordo | Mandate |
|---|---|
| **Ordo Sepulturum** | Plague, undeath, corpse cults, bio-contagion |
| **Ordo Chronos** | Time anomalies, erased histories, causal heresy |
| **Ordo Scriptorum** | Forbidden texts, memetic corruption, archive heresy |
| **Ordo Sicarius** | Imperial assassins, unauthorized killings, kill-orders |
| **Ordo Machinum** | Heretek cults, scrapcode, forbidden machine intelligence |

**Default Red Line:** Never let outsiders learn this Ordo exists.  
**Ordo Move — Narrow Expertise:** Once per Mission, gain Mastery or a D8 Resource when dealing with the Ordo's specific threat. Outside that threat, all Patron Requisitions are Risky.

## 5. Philosophy

The Patron's Philosophy defines how they believe the Inquisition should win.

Choose a public Philosophy. Then choose a private Method. These may match, but the best Patrons usually have tension between them.

| Philosophy | Belief | Mechanical Pressure |
|---|---|---|
| **Puritan** | The enemy must be destroyed without compromise. | Gain Mastery when refusing forbidden aid; Radical methods mark Patron Notice. |
| **Radical** | The enemy's tools may be necessary. | Forbidden Methods are easier to justify; Puritan authorities become more dangerous. |
| **Amalathian** | Stability preserves the Imperium. | Reduce Heat by protecting institutions; lose Patience when institutions are destabilized. |
| **Recongregator** | Rotten systems must be broken and remade. | Gain resources from disruption; lose Patience when the cell preserves corrupt authority. |
| **Monodominant** | Anything non-human or impure must be destroyed. | Mercy toward mutants, xenos, or witches risks Authority stress. |
| **Xanthite** | Warp tools can be turned against the Warp. | Daemonological methods gain power but create Corruption evidence. |
| **Oblationist** | Damnation is acceptable if humanity survives. | Severe Radical Beats impress the Patron; Humanity Beats may be seen as weakness. |
| **Istvaanian** | Conflict makes the Imperium stronger. | Escalation may be rewarded; de-escalation may displease the Patron. |

### Private Method

This is what the Patron actually does when no one is watching.

| Private Method | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Blackmail** | They prefer leverage over law. |
| **Purge** | They solve uncertainty with fire. |
| **Infiltration** | They embed agents everywhere. |
| **Experimentation** | They preserve dangerous things to study them. |
| **Containment** | They seal threats rather than destroy them. |
| **Exploitation** | They use the enemy's tools. |
| **Martyrdom** | They spend lives freely, including yours. |
| **Denial** | They erase records, witnesses, and sometimes entire missions. |

When the cell acts according to the Patron's private Method, they may gain Mastery once per Operation — but mark Patron Notice.

## 6. Mandate

The Mandate is what the Patron is hunting right now.

| D10 | Mandate |
|---:|---|
| 1 | A noble bloodline compromised by heresy |
| 2 | A rogue psyker network hidden in plain sight |
| 3 | A genestealer cult embedded in labor, shipping, or faith networks |
| 4 | A daemonological text moving through Imperial archives |
| 5 | A missing Interrogator carrying forbidden evidence |
| 6 | A xenos relic being quietly exploited by Imperial officials |
| 7 | A shrine-world miracle that should not be possible |
| 8 | A planetary tithe collapse hiding mass sedition |
| 9 | A rival Inquisitor's cell operating in the same theater |
| 10 | A conspiracy inside the Inquisition itself |

### Mandate Rule

When the cell acts directly to advance the Mandate, one acolyte may gain Mastery once per Situation.

When the cell delays, obscures, or contradicts the Mandate, mark Patron Notice.

## 7. Boons

Boons are the Patron's useful assets. Each Boon is a Resource with a die rating.

Starting Patrons have:

- **Limitless Authority** automatically.
- **One Ordo Boon.**
- **One Philosophy Boon.**
- **One chosen Boon.**

Most starting Boons are **D6**. A particularly central or powerful Boon may begin at **D8**.

### Using a Boon

When the cell uses a Boon, choose one:

| Use | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Permission** | The Boon makes an action possible. |
| **Mastery** | The Boon grants Mastery to a relevant roll. |
| **Resource Die** | Roll the Boon's die for effect, protection, leverage, or stress. |
| **Protection** | The Boon absorbs stress like Protection. |
| **Narrative Override** | The Boon solves a logistical obstacle, but causes Patron Notice, Heat, or a Liability trigger. |

After a Boon is used in a major way, the GM may step it down one die size until repaired, justified, concealed, repaid, or refreshed between Missions.

### Core Boon: Limitless Authority

**Limitless Authority — D8 Authority Resource**

Your Patron is an Inquisitor. Officially, legitimate Imperial authorities should answer their call.

Once per Operation, the cell may invoke Limitless Authority to do one:

- Gain Mastery on Command, Procure, or Investigate.
- Requisition aid from an Imperial faction.
- Bypass legal or bureaucratic restrictions.
- Force an official to comply.
- Seize a person, place, record, or object under Inquisitorial mandate.

Then choose one cost:

- Mark D6 Authority stress.
- Mark D6 Shadow stress.
- Increase Heat by 1.
- Trigger a Patron Liability.
- Mark Patron Notice.

## 8. Example Boons

### Spy Network

**D8 Shadow or Investigation Resource**

Use to ask who is watching a target, gain Mastery on surveillance or counter-surveillance, create a D6 Contact, or reduce Heat by 1 if the network can redirect suspicion.

Cost: If compromised, suffer Shadow fallout or trigger **Enemy Operatives**.

### Restricted Archive

**D8 Inquisition or Forbidden Knowledge Resource**

Use to ask one true question about a known threat category, gain Mastery on Investigate + Heresy/Warp/Xenos/Inquisition, or identify a pattern from a previous case.

Cost: Each use risks D3 Mind, Authority, or Corruption stress.

### Cover Story

**D6 or D8 Shadow Resource**

Use to gain Shadow Protection 1 for the Operation, create a D6 Cover Identity for each acolyte, or convert a failed Deceive roll into success at cost.

Cost: If the Cover burns, Heat increases by 1 and the Patron marks Notice.

### Sanctum Obscurus

**D8 Safehouse Resource**

Use to lay low, store prisoners/artifacts/corpses/forbidden evidence, treat fallout, or restore one Boon die step.

Cost: If discovered, suffer Severe Shadow fallout.

### Adeptus Arbites Ties

**D8 Authority Resource**

Use to create a cordon, arrest a suspect, seize records, suppress unrest, or provide armed backup.

Cost: Heat increases by 1 if the Arbites appear openly. If overused, local law begins investigating the cell.

### Kill-Team Sanction

**D10 Violence Resource**

Use to destroy a target, hold a location, extract the cell, or overwhelm mundane opposition.

Cost: Heat increases by 2. Evidence is destroyed. Survivors remember the Inquisition.

### Voidship Access

**D8 Void Resource**

Use to escape a planet or station, move unseen, scan from orbit, quarantine a location, or insert the cell under false credentials.

Cost: The ship's captain, navigator, or owner now has leverage.

### Forbidden Archive

**D8 Corruption / Knowledge Resource**

Use to ask a true question about daemons, xenos, sorcery, or heretek devices, gain Mastery with a Forbidden Method, or identify how to exploit a threat.

Cost: Always take D3 Mind or Corruption stress.

### Plausible Deniability

**D8 Shadow Resource**

Use to reduce Heat by 1 after a Situation, downgrade Shadow fallout by one severity, or erase minor evidence of the cell's presence.

Cost: Someone innocent, useful, or loyal takes the blame.

### Forbidden Specialist

**D6 or D8 Asset**

The Patron can provide a bound daemonologist, sanctioned witch, xenos savant, repentant cultist, Jokaero technician, heretek prisoner, or mutant oracle.

Use to gain Mastery in their specialty, ask a dangerous question, or unlock a forbidden option.

Cost: If exposed, trigger Authority or Corruption fallout.

## 9. Liabilities

Liabilities are the price of the Patron's power.

A Patron starts with one Liability for each Boon.

Some Liabilities are known to the cell. Others are secret until triggered.

### Triggering a Liability

Once per Operation, the GM may trigger a relevant Liability when:

- The cell uses a Patron Boon.
- The cell invokes Limitless Authority.
- The cell crosses a Red Line.
- Heat reaches 6 or higher.
- A player completes a Major or Severe Radical Beat.
- The cell suffers Severe Authority, Shadow, or Corruption fallout.

When a Liability triggers, choose one:

| Liability Effect | Result |
|---|---|
| **Complicate** | A roll becomes Risky or Dangerous. |
| **Expose** | Heat increases by 1. |
| **Pressure** | Mark D6 Authority, Shadow, Mind, or Corruption stress. |
| **Consume** | Step down one Boon die. |
| **Demand** | The Patron issues an immediate order. |
| **Reveal** | A secret, rival, or hidden cost enters play. |

### Example Liabilities

| Liability | Trigger |
|---|---|
| **Demands Discretion** | Public authority use, open violence, or witness trail. |
| **Hidden Philosophy** | The cell discovers the Patron's methods do not match their public doctrine. |
| **Enemy Inquisitor** | Rival agents appear, block requisitions, or reinterpret the mission. |
| **Paranoid** | The Patron audits the cell after unexplained success or missing information. |
| **Loose Cannon** | The Patron escalates without warning. |
| **Offended Faction** | Mechanicus, Ecclesiarchy, Arbites, Navy, or noble allies refuse cooperation. |
| **Sanction Denied** | The Patron cannot legally provide the requested asset. |
| **Tainted Asset** | A Boon is corrupt, compromised, xenos-touched, or daemon-marked. |
| **Meticulous Archivist** | Missing records become Authority stress. |
| **Rumoured Dead** | The Patron's authority is doubted or contested. |
| **Suspect** | Other Inquisitors already distrust the Patron. |
| **Debts Owed** | Every major Requisition creates a favor owed to someone worse. |
| **Nemesis** | A specific enemy knows the Patron's methods and watches for them. |
| **Hallowed Ignorance** | The Patron forbids certain kinds of knowledge, even when useful. |
| **Plausible Betrayal** | The Patron may disavow the cell to protect the larger mission. |

## 10. Patron Notice

Patron Notice measures how much attention the cell has drawn from their own master.

Mark **1 Patron Notice** when:

- A Major or Severe Beat is completed.
- The cell publicly invokes Inquisitorial authority.
- A Patron Boon is used loudly or dramatically.
- Heat reaches 3, 6, or 9 for the first time in an Operation.
- The cell suffers Severe Authority, Shadow, or Corruption fallout.
- The cell delays or contradicts the Mandate.
- A Red Line is approached.

When Patron Notice reaches **3**, the Patron makes a Move, then clear Patron Notice.

| D10 | Patron Move |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Commendation:** Gain a D8 Requisition for the next Operation. |
| 2 | **Correction:** The Patron issues a new restriction or Red Line. |
| 3 | **Audit:** A savant, Interrogator, servo-skull, confessor, or rival acolyte observes the cell. |
| 4 | **Escalation:** The Patron reveals the threat is worse than stated. Heat increases by 1. |
| 5 | **Demand:** The Patron requires a prisoner, artifact, confession, corpse, clean record, or scapegoat. |
| 6 | **Suspicion:** Each acolyte who completed a Radical Beat marks D3 Authority or Shadow stress. |
| 7 | **Doctrine:** The Patron forbids one method and endorses another. |
| 8 | **Requisition Freeze:** One Boon cannot be used until the cell explains itself. |
| 9 | **Rival Cell:** Another cell is assigned to the same theater. They may help, compete, or clean up. |
| 10 | **Forbidden Offer:** The Patron offers a powerful but compromising solution. |

## 11. Patience

Patience is how much failure, disobedience, exposure, and ideological drift the Patron will tolerate.

| Patron Type | Starting Patience |
|---|---:|
| Trusting, distant, overcommitted | 5 |
| Professional, pragmatic | 4 |
| Harsh, orthodox, suspicious | 3 |
| Paranoid, radical, politically vulnerable | 2 |

Lose **1 Patience** when:

- The cell crosses a Red Line.
- Heat reaches 9.
- The cell hides major evidence from the Patron and is discovered.
- A Patron Boon is lost, corrupted, or publicly exposed.
- An acolyte suffers Critical Corruption or Authority fallout.
- A rival Inquisitor gains leverage over the Patron.
- The cell disobeys a direct order.

When Patience reaches **0**, make a Judgment Move.

| D6 | Judgment |
|---:|---|
| 1 | **Reindoctrination:** One acolyte is recalled, conditioned, punished, or spiritually examined. |
| 2 | **Disavowal:** The cell loses Limitless Authority until they restore trust. |
| 3 | **Sacrificial Order:** The Patron gives an order that will cost a Bond, Cover, or Resource. |
| 4 | **Replacement Cell:** A rival team arrives with authority to supersede the PCs. |
| 5 | **Purge Protocol:** The Patron authorizes force against the cell's assets, witnesses, or allies. |
| 6 | **Excommunication Threat:** The next Severe Authority or Corruption fallout may become Critical. |

Patience can be restored by completing a major Mandate objective, delivering something the Patron demanded, accepting punishment, exposing a rival, or sacrificing a valuable asset for the mission.

## 12. Red Lines

Red Lines are things the cell must not do — or must never be caught doing.

Each Patron has three:

1. **Do not do this.**
2. **Do not be seen doing this.**
3. **Do not let anyone learn I ordered this.**

| Red Line | If Crossed |
|---|---|
| Do not reveal the Inquisition's presence. | Mark Patron Notice and Heat +1. |
| Do not use xenos artifacts. | Mark Authority or Corruption stress. |
| Do not destroy the target before interrogation. | Lose 1 Patience. |
| Do not involve local authorities. | One Boon becomes unavailable. |
| Do not let the Ecclesiarchy control the narrative. | Trigger Offended Faction or Demands Discretion. |
| Do not preserve daemonological evidence. | Severe Authority fallout if discovered. |
| Do not expose the Patron's name. | Heat jumps to 6 or 9 depending on scale. |
| Do not kill nobles without proof. | Rival faction begins investigation. |
| Do not show mercy to the infected. | Humanity Beats risk Patron Notice. |
| Do not let the cell become publicly celebrated. | Plausible Deniability triggers. |

## 13. Standing Order

A Standing Order is the Patron's default command.

| Standing Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Preserve Subtlety Above All** | When Heat rises, mark Patron Notice. |
| **Bring Me Prisoners** | Killing key suspects risks Authority stress. |
| **Burn the Taint** | Preserving forbidden evidence marks Patron Notice. |
| **Find the Mastermind** | Ending an Operation without identifying higher links loses Patience. |
| **Protect Imperial Institutions** | Exposing official corruption is Risky unless framed carefully. |
| **Trust No Local Authority** | Requisition from locals triggers Liability on 7 or lower. |
| **The Cell Is Expendable** | Once per Mission, ignore a Patron Notice mark, but someone suffers. |
| **Truth Before Stability** | Cover-ups and scapegoats risk Mind or Authority stress. |

When an acolyte acts in clear obedience to the Standing Order, they may gain Mastery once per Operation.

When the cell openly violates it, mark Patron Notice.

---

## 14. Forbidden Offer

A Forbidden Offer is the Patron's tempting solution. It is powerful, effective, and incriminating.

| Forbidden Offer | Benefit | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Use the daemonological index.** | Ask one true question about the Warp. | D6 Corruption or Mind stress. |
| **Deploy the unsanctioned psyker.** | Gain D8 psychic Resource. | Authority or Corruption fallout if exposed. |
| **Plant false evidence.** | Redirect suspicion or reduce Heat. | Someone innocent becomes endangered. |
| **Use the xenos weapon.** | Gain D8 or D10 offensive Resource. | Corruption, Authority, or Mechanicus attention. |
| **Erase the witnesses.** | Reduce Heat by 2. | Humanity Beat opportunity; Mind stress. |
| **Call the kill-team.** | Destroy a threat. | Heat +2 and evidence destroyed. |
| **Release the prisoner.** | They lead you to the larger conspiracy. | They will hurt someone first. |
| **Let the cult complete the rite.** | Expose the true master. | Dangerous or Impossible Situation follows. |

The Forbidden Offer should always work. That is why it is dangerous.

## 15. Patron Requisition Procedure

When the cell asks the Patron for a resource, decide whether it is **Routine**, **Costly**, or **Contested**.

| Type | Procedure |
|---|---|
| **Routine** | The Patron provides it if a relevant Boon exists. |
| **Costly** | The Patron provides it, but mark Patron Notice, step down a Boon, or take Authority/Shadow stress. |
| **Contested** | Roll Procure + relevant Domain. On 8+, get it. On 6-7, get it with cost. On 2-5, get a lesser version or trigger a Liability. On 1, trigger a Liability and mark Patron Notice. |

Use **Contested** when the request is urgent, illegal, forbidden, faction-dependent, under scrutiny, distant, politically constrained, or likely to expose the cell.

---

## 16. Defying the Patron

When the cell defies a direct order, choose how they do it.

| Method | Roll |
|---|---|
| Hide the disobedience | Deceive + Inquisition |
| Justify the disobedience | Command + Inquisition |
| Outmaneuver the order | Investigate + Imperium |
| Endure the consequences | Resist + Inquisition |

On 8+, the cell gets away with it for now.

On 6-7, they get away with it but mark Patron Notice.

On 2-5, the Patron learns enough to act.

On 1, lose 1 Patience and trigger an immediate Patron Move.

## 17. Patron Refresh

At the end of each Mission, hold a **Debrief**.

Each acolyte answers one:

- What did you accomplish?
- What did you conceal?
- What did you lose?
- What did you do that the Patron would approve of?
- What did you do that the Patron would condemn?
- What proof do you bring?

Then choose one if justified:

| Debrief Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Clean Success** | Clear Patron Notice. |
| **Useful Success** | Restore one Boon by one die step. |
| **Costly Success** | Restore 1 Patience, but trigger a Liability. |
| **Hidden Failure** | Clear Heat or Shadow pressure, but mark Patron Notice. |
| **Condemned Success** | Gain a Requisition, but lose 1 Patience. |
| **Radical Success** | Gain a Forbidden Offer for next Mission. |

## 18. Example Patron

### Inquisitor Severine Vhal, Ordo Hereticus

**Public Philosophy:** Amalathian  
**Private Method:** Blackmail  
**Mandate:** Discover why Hive Carcosa's tithe has collapsed.  
**Standing Order:** Preserve Imperial institutions until the true infection is known.  
**Forbidden Offer:** Plant evidence implicating a minor noble house to flush out the real conspirators.

**Boons**

1. **Limitless Authority — D8**
2. **Spy Network — D8**
3. **Cover Story — D6**
4. **Restricted Archive — D6**

**Liabilities**

1. **Demands Discretion**
2. **Enemy Inquisitor**
3. **Meticulous Archivist**
4. **Hidden Philosophy**

**Patience:** 4  
**Patron Notice:** 0 / 3

**Red Lines**

1. Do not reveal the Inquisition's presence in Hive Carcosa.
2. Do not kill senior nobles without proof.
3. Do not let the Ecclesiarchy declare a public witch-hunt before the tithe conspiracy is mapped.

**Secret:** Vhal already knows one noble house is guilty. She does not know whether they are the root of the heresy or bait planted by a rival Inquisitor.

## 19. Playtest Intent

This Patron system should create three pressures:

| Pressure | How It Appears |
|---|---|
| **Use the Patron** | Boons, Requisitions, authority, cover, archives, specialists. |
| **Fear the Patron** | Notice, Patience, Red Lines, Judgment Moves. |
| **Question the Patron** | Secret Method, Forbidden Offer, Liabilities, rival Inquisitors. |

The Patron should never be only a quest-giver. They should be a loaded weapon pointed in both directions.
