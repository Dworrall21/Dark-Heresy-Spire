# Dark Heresy Spire: Project Index

A fan design project: **Spire's Resistance Toolbox-style pressure engine rebuilt for an Imperium Maledictum / Inquisition campaign.**

> Status: working draft / playtest text.  
> This repository contains original fan design notes and rules text only. It does not reproduce the source rulebooks.

## Core Idea

You are acolytes of the Holy Orders of the Emperor's Inquisition.

You are not heroes. You are not soldiers in a clean war. You are deniable instruments of a terrible authority, sent to uncover threats the Imperium cannot publicly admit exist.

Your task is to investigate, infiltrate, judge, and purge.

The game asks:

**What are you willing to risk to learn the truth, keep your cover, obey your master, and survive the thing you uncover?**

## Design Pillars

### Investigation before combat

The game should reward finding patterns, building networks, exploiting leverage, and deciding when to strike. Combat is not a separate tactical minigame; it is one way a situation becomes costly.

### Authority is both weapon and liability

Inquisitorial authority can requisition troops, override officials, silence witnesses, and terrify civilians. But every use burns cover, creates enemies, or draws scrutiny from rival powers.

### Subtlety matters

Acolytes are most effective when nobody knows they exist. The moment the cell becomes public, the mission clock accelerates.

### Corruption is useful

Forbidden tools, xenos bargains, warp insight, daemonology, unsanctioned psykers, and radical methods should often be the fastest way to win. That is why they are dangerous.

### The Patron is not your friend

The Inquisitor is the mission-giver, resource source, ideological pressure, and eventual judge. Their Boons and Liabilities should shape the whole campaign.

### Fallout drives the story forward

Fallout should not just punish. It should expose new truths, burn assets, force moral choices, summon auditors, mutate witnesses, compromise cover identities, and make the conspiracy react.
