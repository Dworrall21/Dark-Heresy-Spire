# Character Creation v0.1

This section builds acolytes as fast, thematic, Spire-style characters: defined by role, pressure, bonds, cover, and special abilities rather than granular skill math.

At the end of creation, every acolyte should answer three questions:

1. **Why did the Inquisition choose you?**
2. **What makes you useful?**
3. **What makes you dangerous to keep alive?**

---

## Acolyte Creation Summary

To create an acolyte:

1. Choose an **Origin**.
2. Choose a **Former Allegiance**.
3. Choose a **Class**.
4. Define your **Patron Relationship**.
5. Create a **Cover Identity**.
6. Choose a **Forbidden Edge**.
7. Create **Bonds**.
8. Choose starting **Skills and Domains**.
9. Choose starting **Gear and Protection**.
10. Write your **Refresh Action**.
11. Answer your **Cell Questions**.

---

## 1. Origin

Your Origin is where you came from before the Inquisition found you.

Choose one. Your Origin gives you:

- 1 Domain
- 1 suggested Bond
- 1 question

| Origin | Domain | Suggested Bond | Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hive World** | Underworld or Imperium | A gang, hab-family, manufactorum crew, or local enforcer | Who still knows the person you used to be? |
| **Forge World** | Mechanicus | A tech-priest, forge guild, servitor-handler, or machine cult | What machine did you once fail, offend, or save? |
| **Shrine World** | Ecclesiarchy | A confessor, relic-keeper, pilgrim family, or local saint cult | What holy story do you no longer fully believe? |
| **Voidborn** | Void | A ship crew, navigator's servant, dock contact, or voidsman | What did you see in the dark between stars? |
| **Death World** | Militarum or Frontier | A survivor-clan, beast-hunter, or evacuation officer | What killed everyone weaker than you? |
| **Warzone** | Militarum | A squadmate, commissar, medic, or enemy deserter | Which battle should have killed you? |
| **Schola Progenium** | Imperium or Ecclesiarchy | A drill-abbot, rival cadet, storm trooper, or orphan-sibling | What lesson did they beat into you? |
| **Penal World** | Underworld | A cellmate, handler, former victim, or penal legion contact | What sentence did the Inquisition interrupt? |
| **Frontier World** | Frontier | A settlement elder, smuggler, missionary, or mutant outcast | What did Imperial law fail to understand? |
| **Noble House** | Imperium | A relative, tutor, rival heir, servant, or house spy | What family secret could still ruin you? |

## 2. Former Allegiance

Your Former Allegiance is who trained, owned, condemned, sponsored, or discarded you before the Inquisition claimed you.

Choose one. It gives you:

- 1 Skill
- 1 starting gear option
- 1 institutional complication

| Former Allegiance | Skill | Gear | Complication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adeptus Administratum** | Investigate or Deceive | Seal-stamps, ledger access, cogitator slate | You are still in a system somewhere. |
| **Adeptus Arbites / Enforcers** | Command or Fight | Shock maul, manacles, precinct contact | Local law thinks you are either competition or deserter. |
| **Adeptus Mechanicus** | Operate | Auspex, mechadendrite tool, sacred toolkit | Flesh-loyal authorities distrust your machine loyalties. |
| **Adeptus Ministorum** | Command or Resist | Censer, reliquary token, preacher's robes | Your faith has witnesses. |
| **Astra Militarum** | Fight or Move | Lasgun, flak armour, campaign medal | Someone expects you to still follow orders. |
| **Astra Telepathica** | Resist or Investigate | Psy-focus, sanctioning brand, warded hood | Everyone is waiting for you to become a liability. |
| **Navis / Void Guild** | Operate or Skulk | Void suit, ship pass, emergency beacon | Your access depends on old void contracts. |
| **Rogue Trader Retinue** | Procure or Deceive | Exotic trinket, trade writ, xenos rumor | You know too much about tolerated illegality. |
| **Underworld Asset** | Skulk or Deceive | Hidden blade, false papers, black-market contact | The law has not forgotten you. |
| **Noble Household** | Command or Procure | Signet, dueling weapon, etiquette training | Your bloodline can open doors or draw knives. |
| **Cult Survivor / Penitent** | Resist or Investigate | Scarred scripture, old cult token, penance brand | Someone doubts you were truly cleansed. |
| **Ordo-Trained Initiate** | Investigate or Command | Interrogation notes, cipher-key, restricted primer | Rival acolytes know your early failures. |

## 3. Class

Your Class is what you do for the cell. It gives your strongest identity and most of your special rules.

Choose one.

| Class | Concept |
|---|---|
| **Interrogator** | The Patron's heir-apparent, trusted but watched |
| **Explicator** | Pattern-reader, clue-breaker, savant investigator |
| **Cruciator** | Torturer, confessor, coercive truth-maker |
| **Penumbra** | Spy, infiltrator, false-face specialist |
| **Sanctioned Psyker** | Weaponized liability, occult sensor, warp conduit |
| **Exorcist** | Anti-daemon specialist, ward-maker, ritualist |
| **Hierophant** | Priest, zealot, morale engine, doctrinal judge |
| **Secutor** | Killer, bodyguard, stormtrooper, raid leader |
| **Chirurgeon** | Medicae, vivisectionist, plague-handler, flesh expert |
| **Enginseer** | Tech-priest, machine-speaker, heretek detector |
| **Untouchable** | Blank/pariah, anti-warp anchor, social horror |
| **Radical Asset** | Former cultist, xenos-touched survivor, sanctioned monster |

Each Class gives:

- 1 Core Ability
- 1 Refresh Action
- 1 Skill
- 1 Domain
- 1 Protection
- Starting gear
- Class questions
- Minor, Major, and Severe advances

## 4. Patron Relationship

Your Patron is not just the mission-giver. They are your protection, your judge, your resource pipeline, and your executioner.

Choose why your Patron keeps you.

| Patron Relationship | Benefit | Pressure |
|---|---|---|
| **Favored Instrument** | Once per Mission, gain Mastery when following direct orders. | If you disobey, mark D6 Authority stress. |
| **Expendable Asset** | Once per Operation, ignore Authority stress from collateral damage. | Your Patron will not protect you from fallout. |
| **Blackmailed Servant** | Gain Shadow Protection 1. | Someone has proof of your old sin. |
| **Potential Interrogator** | Gain Authority Protection 1. | You are judged more harshly than the others. |
| **Redeemed Heretic** | Gain Corruption Protection 1. | Any Corruption fallout is treated as evidence. |
| **Borrowed Specialist** | Gain one extra Skill. | Your former faction expects reports. |
| **Secret Weapon** | Gain one Psychic Power or Forbidden Method. | Your existence must remain hidden. |
| **Political Liability** | Gain one extra Resource at the start of each Mission. | If exposed, your Patron may disavow you. |

## 5. Cover Identity

Every acolyte begins with one Cover Identity.

A Cover Identity is a Resource die that can be used when it helps you pass as someone else, access locations, avoid questions, or explain your presence.

Choose a die:

- **D6 Cover** if it is plausible but limited.
- **D8 Cover** if it is well-supported and useful.
- **D10 Cover** only if your whole class or Patron setup centers on it.

Most starting characters should begin with **D6 Cover**.

| Cover Identity | Good For |
|---|---|
| Administratum tithe auditor | Records, bureaucracy, noble access |
| Munitorum logistics officer | Warzones, supplies, troop movement |
| Pilgrim confessor | Shrines, common folk, religious spaces |
| Chartist vessel factor | Ports, void stations, cargo manifests |
| Manufactorum safety inspector | Forge sites, labor blocks, machine rooms |
| Underhive narcotics buyer | Gangs, smugglers, recidivists |
| Rogue Trader factor | Exotic goods, xenos rumors, noble courts |
| Corpse reclamation officer | Bodies, plagues, disappearances |
| Sanctioned bounty agent | Violence, warrants, low-level authority |
| Ecclesiarchy relic examiner | Reliquaries, saints, cults, archives |

### Burning Cover

You may burn your Cover Identity to:

- Ignore one Shadow fallout.
- Gain automatic access to a place your cover could plausibly reach.
- Convert a failed Deceive or Skulk roll into success at cost.

When you burn Cover:

- The Cover is destroyed.
- Heat increases by 1.
- Someone remembers enough to matter later.

## 6. Forbidden Edge

Your Forbidden Edge is the thing that makes you valuable and dangerous.

Choose one.

| Forbidden Edge | Benefit | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Warp-Sensitive** | Begin with Witch-Sight. | Take +1 Corruption stress from psychic backlash. |
| **Xenos-Touched** | Gain Xenos as a Domain. | Possession of your knowledge is incriminating. |
| **Former Cultist** | Gain Heresy as a Domain. | Cults may recognize you, and authorities may distrust you. |
| **Radical Training** | Begin with one Forbidden Method. | Puritan scrutiny treats you as suspicious. |
| **Blank Blood** | Gain Corruption Protection 1. | Comforting or inspiring others is Risky when your nature is known. |
| **Hypno-Indoctrinated** | Gain Mind Protection 1. | The GM may introduce sealed orders or memory gaps. |
| **Daemon-Scarred** | Gain Mastery when identifying daemonic influence. | Daemons may identify you in return. |
| **Heretek Exposure** | Gain Mechanicus or Xenos. | Machine authorities may want to dissect your knowledge. |
| **Unregistered Asset Network** | Begin with a D6 Contact. | The Contact is illegal, compromised, or hunted. |
| **Penance Brand** | Gain Authority Protection 1 when confessing guilt. | Your brand marks you as previously condemned. |

## 7. Bonds

Bonds are people, institutions, secrets, or obligations that matter to you.

Create three Bonds:

1. One Bond to another acolyte.
2. One Bond to someone outside the cell.
3. One Bond to a faction, place, secret, or former life.

Each Bond has:

- Name
- Why they matter
- What they can provide
- What would put them at risk

| Bond | Can Provide | At Risk From |
|---|---|---|
| Former squadmate | Safehouse, weapons, battlefield rumor | Militarum discipline, guilt, exposure |
| Hive sibling | Local knowledge, emotional anchor | Shadow fallout, hostage-taking |
| Confessor | Absolution, contacts, doctrine | Corruption evidence |
| Tech-priest colleague | Repairs, access, machine lore | Heretek suspicion |
| Underhive fixer | Contraband, rumors, passage | Betrayal, gang politics |
| Rival acolyte | Inquisition rumor, warning, leverage | Authority fallout |
| Saved mutant child | Humanity, local trust, hidden paths | Puritan judgment |
| Noble cousin | Access, money, invitations | Family scandal |
| Bound familiar | Scouting, sensory link, warning | Capture, corruption |
| Dead mentor's notes | Clues, forbidden history | Rival Inquisitors |

### Bond Stress

Bonds can take stress like Resources.

A Bond usually has a die rating:

- **D6 Bond:** useful but fragile
- **D8 Bond:** reliable or influential
- **D10 Bond:** powerful, rare, or deeply important

When a Bond is harmed, compromised, exhausted, or endangered, reduce its die by one step. If it drops below D3, the Bond breaks, dies, betrays you, disappears, or becomes a threat.

## 8. Starting Skills and Domains

After Origin, Former Allegiance, and Class, each acolyte should have:

- 3 Skills
- 3 Domains

You gain:

- 1 Domain from Origin
- 1 Skill from Former Allegiance
- 1 Skill and 1 Domain from Class
- Choose 1 extra Skill
- Choose 1 extra Domain

No starting acolyte may begin with more than **2 Forbidden Domains** from this list unless their Class allows it:

- Heresy
- Warp
- Xenos

## 10. Refresh Action

Your Refresh Action is how you reduce stress by acting in accordance with who you are.

When you perform your Refresh Action, remove stress from one Resistance:

| Refresh Strength | Stress Removed |
|---|---|
| Minor refresh | D3 |
| Significant refresh | D6 |
| Dramatic refresh | D8 |

Your Class gives a default Refresh, but you should personalize it.

Examples:

- Remove stress when you confess what you have done.
- Remove stress when you burn a false identity and walk away.
- Remove stress when you prove a forbidden method was necessary.
- Remove stress when you repair a machine that should have been abandoned.
- Remove stress when you comfort someone doctrine says should be condemned.
- Remove stress when you execute a threat before it can become a tragedy.
- Remove stress when you tell the truth to someone powerful.
- Remove stress when you submit to examination, restraint, or penance.

## 11. Cell Questions

After everyone makes acolytes, answer these together.

### The Patron

1. Who is your Patron?
2. Which Ordo do they claim?
3. What Philosophy do they publicly follow?
4. What method do they privately tolerate?
5. What is their Red Line?
6. What would make them disavow you?

### The Cell

1. How long have you worked together?
2. Which acolyte has saved another's life?
3. Which acolyte knows another's secret?
4. Which acolyte distrusts the Patron most?
5. Which acolyte is most likely to cross the line?
6. What mission still haunts the cell?

### The Current Mission

1. What world, hive, shrine, station, or warzone are you entering?
2. What is the official reason you are there?
3. What is the real suspicion?
4. Why can't the Patron intervene directly?
5. What local authority must not learn the truth?
6. What happens if the cell fails?

## 13. Example Character

### Acolyte Mara Venn

**Origin:** Hive World  
**Former Allegiance:** Adeptus Administratum  
**Class:** Explicator  
**Patron Relationship:** Potential Interrogator  
**Cover Identity:** Administratum tithe auditor, D6  
**Forbidden Edge:** Former Cultist

**Skills:** Investigate, Deceive, Procure  
**Domains:** Underworld, Imperium, Heresy

**Protection:** Mind Protection 1, Authority Protection 1

**Gear:** Laspistol; cipher slate; evidence kit; old cult token hidden in a prayer-book.

**Bonds:**

- Acolyte Bond: Secutor Vale once spared her during a purge.
- External Bond: Her younger brother still works in Hive Tarsus's corpse starch office.
- Faction / Secret Bond: The cult she betrayed believes she died in the purge.

**Core Ability — The Shape of Guilt:** When Mara finds a clue, she may ask one extra question: “What does this imply that no one wants noticed?” Take D3 Mind stress if the answer points toward someone powerful.

**Refresh Action:** Remove stress when Mara explains the truth to someone who does not want to hear it.

**Starting Problem:** Mara knows the cult's old recognition phrases still work. She has not told the cell.
