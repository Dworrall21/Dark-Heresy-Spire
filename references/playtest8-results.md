POST-PLAYTEST ANALYSIS
======================================================================

WHAT WORKED WELL:
  1. Psychic power procedure is clean: roll, check <=7 for Corruption,
     check for 1/Burden, apply Warp Trace. The 7-step process in the
     rules maps directly to code.
  2. Forbidden Method result bands (10/8-9/6-7/2-5/1) create clear
     escalation. The GM always knows what to do with the result.
  3. Warp Trace as a shared clock (not per-character) creates cell-level
     tension. Every psychic use risks collective exposure.
  4. Daemonhost with multi-Resistance Protection forces the cell to
     break wards BEFORE dealing damage. This creates a satisfying
     two-phase combat that rewards preparation.
  5. Pyra's Corruption 5 (lowest Resistance) creates genuine tension.
     Every psychic use risks her. The tragic archetype works.

FRICTION POINTS & RULES ISSUES:
  1. IMPOSSIBLE DIFFICULTY + SMALL POOL: Pyra rolling 4D10 Impossible
     -> 2D10 means she's rolling 2 dice against a system designed for
     3-5 dice. At 2D10, P(highest>=8) = 1 - P(both<=7) = 1-(0.7^2)
     = 51% for Clean/Crit. That's actually reasonable for a desperate
     last stand. BUT: if the pool were 3D10 -> 1D10 after Impossible,
     P(success) = 40%. One die. That's a coin flip at best.
     -> FINDING: Impossible on pools <=3 is punishing but appropriate
     for 'cannot work cleanly' situations. The system handles this OK.

  2. CORRUPTION SPIRAL: Pyra took Corruption stress on 4 of 5 psychic
     rolls (all but the first Safe roll). Her Corruption Resistance is 5.
     She triggered Moderate Corruption Fallout once. If the mission
     were one operation longer, she'd likely hit Severe.
     -> FINDING: The Corruption spiral IS working as designed. The
     question is whether players find it fun or just punishing.
     The Burden system (1-in-20 per roll) adds narrative variety.

  3. FORBIDDEN ASSET STEP-DOWN: The Grimoire went D10->D8 in one use.
     At this rate, it's gone in 4 uses. That feels fast for a major
     asset. But it IS a daemonological text — it should be dangerous.
     -> FINDING: The step-down rate is appropriate for forbidden assets.
     They're powerful but consumable. This creates good resource tension.

  4. WARP TRACE CLOCK: Advanced 5 times across the mission (Pyra's
     Witch-Sight + Veil + 2x Rebuke + Soul Hook). At 5/6, one more
     psychic use and the clock fills. What happens at 6?
     -> FINDING: The rules don't specify what a full Warp Trace clock
     does. This is a gap. Suggestion: at 6, daemons can track the
     cell's location, or a warp rift opens at their last position.

  5. POSSESSION CLOCK: Only triggered on Critical Failure of the final
     sealing. In this playtest, Pyra succeeded (barely). But the
     possession clock mechanic is untested at 4 segments.
     -> FINDING: Need to define what each segment of the possession
     clock means. Suggestion: 1=whispers, 2=blackouts, 3=body
     hijacking, 4=full possession.

  6. MIND SCRAPE NOT TESTED: Pyra has Mind-Scrape but never used it.
     It's Forbidden and Invasive — the kind of power that should be
     tempting but wasn't needed in this mission structure.
     -> FINDING: The mission didn't create a situation where tearing
     a memory from a target was the obvious solution. Future missions
     should include a 'locked witness' scenario.

  7. BRUTAL TAG ON DAEMONIC ENEMIES: The Possessed Cultist has D8
     stress die. If the cultist had Brutal, that'd be max(D8,D8) =
     average 4.5 but max 8. Same average, higher ceiling. Brutal on
     enemies is a pure upside for the GM — no rolling, just pick
     the higher of two. This is correct per the rules.
     -> FINDING: Brutal on enemies is simple and effective. No issues.

PLAYER MOTIVATION ANALYSIS:
  Pyra (Tragic): Her low Corruption Resistance (5) made every psychic
    use a gamble. She succeeded but accumulated stress rapidly. The
    tragic arc is supported — she's the most powerful and most at risk.
    The Burden system gave her narrative complications (Warp Scent,
    etc.) that will haunt future operations.

  Daven (Winner): His Body Protection 2 and Brutal shotgun made him
    the reliable combat anchor. He took stress but never triggered
    Fallout. The 'prepare or suffer' principle worked — he requisitioned
    gear and it paid off. His burden (contact leverage) is a future hook.

  Lyssa (Combo-Seeker): She used two Forbidden Methods (Heretek Bypass
    + Daemonological Consultation + Memory Work). Each cost stress and
    generated Heat/EA. The Grimoire step-down created resource tension.
    She's the one pushing the system's edges — exactly what this
    archetype should do. Her Corruption 6 is the next pressure point.

OVERALL DESIGN HEALTH:
  The psychic and forbidden method subsystems are well-designed. The
  core loop (roll -> cost -> Warp Trace -> potential Burden -> Fallout)
  creates escalating tension that rewards careful use and punishes
  recklessness. The main gaps are: (1) Warp Trace clock completion
  effects undefined, (2) Possession clock segment effects undefined,
  (3) no mission scenario that exercises memory-ripping powers.
  The system is ready for table play with minor documentation fixes.
